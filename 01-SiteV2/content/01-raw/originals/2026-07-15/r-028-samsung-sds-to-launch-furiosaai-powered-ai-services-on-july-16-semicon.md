# Samsung SDS to Launch FuriosaAI-Powered AI Services on July 16 < Semiconductor < 기사본문 - The Elec Inc.

Samsung SDS to Launch FuriosaAI-Powered AI Services on July 16

Choi Jeong-jin, Executive Vice President at Samsung SDS, presents the company's collaboration with FuriosaAI at the 2026 K-NPU Tech Wave at The Plaza Hotel in Seoul on July 14. (Photo: THE ELEC)
Samsung SDS will launch its proprietary AI services powered by FuriosaAI's neural processing units (NPUs) on July 16, marking one of the first commercial deployments of the Korean AI chipmaker's hardware in enterprise AI services.
Speaking at the 2026 K-NPU Tech Wave event at The Plaza Hotel in Seoul on July 14, Choi Jeong-jin, Executive Vice President at Samsung SDS, said the company will launch its NPU-as-a-Service (NPUaaS) platform based on FuriosaAI's second-generation RNGD (Renegade) NPU.
"We have already secured customers for the Renegade-based NPUaaS service, which will power both FabriX and Brity Works," Choi said.
FabriX is Samsung SDS's enterprise generative AI platform and is currently used for applications including government civil complaint processing. Brity Works is an AI collaboration platform optimized for public-sector and administrative organizations, offering functions such as document search, email management and mobile conferencing.
Renegade is FuriosaAI's AI inference processor designed to deliver higher power efficiency than graphics processing units (GPUs). According to the company, the chip can serve 7.4 times more users than NVIDIA RTX Pro 6000 under comparable conditions. FuriosaAI began mass production of Renegade in the first half of this year.
Samsung SDS had previously announced plans in April to offer NPUaaS through its cloud platform using Renegade chips. Choi said customers will be able to use the service for AI training, inference and serverless AI workloads, in configurations of one, two, four or eight accelerator cards.
The company will deploy Renegade NPUs at its data centers in Sangam, Seoul, and Dongtan, Gyeonggi Province. Choi declined to disclose the number of installed chips but said deployment remains at an early stage and will continue to expand through the end of the year.
Samsung SDS is also evaluating the use of NPUs, rather than GPUs, for its Robot Transformation (RX) business. Choi said the company has not yet selected an NPU supplier and that the initiative remains in the planning stage. The RX business focuses on replacing human labor with robots in smart factories, with NPUs serving as the robots' AI processors.
Choi declined to comment on potential future collaboration with FuriosaAI beyond the current generation, saying it is too early to discuss whether Samsung SDS will offer NPUaaS based on the company's third-generation chip.
At the same event, FuriosaAI disclosed new details about its third-generation AI processor. Kang Ji-hoon, the company's Chief Research Officer (CRO), said the new chip is being designed to match the efficiency of leading AI accelerators used by hyperscale cloud providers.
He added that the third-generation processor will deliver 10 to 30 times the compute capacity of Renegade.
The chip is already under development and is expected to enter mass production as early as the end of 2027. Target applications include large language models (LLMs), agentic AI, CCTV analytics and both data center and on-premises deployments.
The chip will be manufactured by TSMC using a process node more advanced than the 5-nanometer technology used for Renegade, although the company did not disclose the specific node. Industry sources expect it to use a 2-nanometer process.
FuriosaAI has selected Broadcom as the ASIC design partner under a turnkey agreement covering development through tape-out. While the company did not disclose contract terms, industry estimates put the tape-out cost at between 100 billion won and 200 billion won.
Development of the third-generation chip will be funded through the company's recent pre-IPO financing. FuriosaAI is raising capital through redeemable convertible preferred shares (RCPS) priced at approximately 1.7 million won per share.
As of July 14, the company had secured investment commitments totaling about 800 billion won, with roughly 700 billion won scheduled to be received on July 16. FuriosaAI plans to keep the funding round open until the end of July and expects total pre-IPO financing to reach the trillion-won level.
The company is currently valued at about 3 trillion won, with its valuation expected to increase to 3.8 trillion won to 4 trillion won after the funding round concludes.
