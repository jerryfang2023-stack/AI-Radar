Strategies to respond to increased demand for Generative AI inference and increased power costs are attracting attention in the artificial intelligence (AI) infrastructure market. In response, Korean AI companies have introduced domestic neural network processing (NPU)-based services and platform strategies.
At the "2026 K-NPU Tech Wave" held by Puriosa AI and Sisone at The Plaza Hotel in Jung-gu, Seoul on the 14th, Samsung SDS, WiseNut, and Dudaji participated to unveil domestic NPU-based AI infrastructure strategies. At the event, it was predicted that the structure in which the graphics processing unit (GPU) is in charge of AI learning but the NPU is in charge of reasoning will become a new market standard.
Kim Eun-joo, head of the intelligent technology infrastructure division of the National Intelligence Agency (NIA), said in a keynote speech, "As the center of AI competition shifts from learning to reasoning and agents, domestic NPU is a key foundation for securing AI sovereignty," adding, "The leading introduction of the public sector will be the priming water to open the initial market."
Samsung SDS announced that it will officially launch NPU (NPUaaS) as a service based on Samsung Cloud Platform (SCP) on the 16th. It is characterized by allowing companies to use Generative AI inference services by utilizing domestic NPUs in the cloud without building a separate AI infrastructure.
Samsung SDS's NPUaaS is based on Puriosa AI's RNGD. Renegade is Puriosa AI's second-generation NPU product and began mass production in January. Puriosa AI has proposed an NXT Renegade server that can carry up to eight Renegade cards as the core of the AI inference system.
사진 확대
Choi Jeong-jin, head of Samsung SDS Group, is presenting at the 2026 K-NPU Tech Wave. Puriosa AI
"We will provide NPU in the form of virtual machines to help companies reduce power consumption and total cost of ownership (TCO)," said Choi Jeong-jin, head of Samsung SDS Group. "Customers can learn AI models only with data and apply inference services without burdening infrastructure choices."
On the same day, Samsung SDS also released the results of a survey on the use of Generative AI by Korean companies. This is because the demand for AI semiconductors for inference will also expand as the utilization time of Generative AI rises rapidly.
According to a survey of 1,750 executives and employees of domestic companies conducted by the Samsung SDS marketing team in May, they use Generative AI for work for an average of 3.2 hours a day. In November last year, the proportion of using more than two hours a day was 18%, but in May this year, it increased to 72%. 65% of respondents said their work hours were reduced due to the use of Generative AI, and they felt an average of 1 hour and 12 minutes of work saving.
Meanwhile, Puriosa AI, which hosted the event, announced its plan to expand the domestic AI ecosystem, focusing on domestic NPU Renegades specialized in Generative AI reasoning.
"Global companies are currently leading the AI semiconductor market, but if Korean companies build an ecosystem together, they will be able to create new growth engines in AI chips and AI infrastructure," said Baek Joon-ho, CEO of Puriosa AI.
