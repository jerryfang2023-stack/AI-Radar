Challenges and Limitations
While the underlying technology is incredibly capable, enterprise deployment requires careful planning around a few key operational realities:
Managing Model Hallucinations: Generative systems can occasionally create inaccurate information. Businesses must secure their voice setups by anchoring agents strictly to real-time database lookups using Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) and robust prompt guardrails.
Handling Complex, Emotional Conversations: AI excels at structured transactions but lacks true human empathy. Systems must be built to recognize escalating customer frustration and route those calls immediately to experienced human agents.
Data Security & Compliance: Phone calls often touch sensitive data. Voice platforms must adhere to strict regulatory frameworks including GDPR, CCPA, HIPAA for medical groups, and PCI-DSS for credit transactions.
Legacy Software Integrations: Older on-premise telephony networks can be difficult to connect. Modern deployments rely on clean, cloud-hosted platforms or secure SIP trunking protocols to link AI engines directly to existing lines.
