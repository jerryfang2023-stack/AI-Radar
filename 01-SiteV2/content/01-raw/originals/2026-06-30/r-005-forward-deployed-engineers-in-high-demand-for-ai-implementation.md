# Forward-Deployed Engineers in High Demand for AI Implementation

## clean_text

Forward-Deployed Engineers (FDEs) are having a moment.
OpenAI, Anthropic, and Cohere are scrambling to hire engineers who can do something truly radical: code AND talk to humans.
The numbers:
FDE job postings up 800% (Jan-Sept 2025)
OpenAI scaling to ~50 FDEs this year
Anthropic growing applied AI team 5x
Palantir Technologies's FDEs now represent half their workforce
Why this matters:
Companies are drowning in AI hype but starving for implementation help. A Fortune 500 bank has completely different needs than a startup building AI-native products.
Turns out, shipping a model API and saying "good luck!" isn't a business strategy.
The best example:
OpenAI embedded FDEs with John Deere to build precision farming tools. Result? Farmers reduced chemical spraying by 60-70%.
That didn't happen because GPT-4 is magic. It happened because someone sat on a tractor and figured out what farmers actually needed.
Here's what's fascinating:
This role came from Palantir Technologies, who literally borrowed it from the military (forward deployment = sending soldiers to foreign bases).
Your AI procurement meeting probably went like this:
"We bought the GPT-4 API!"
"Great! What are we building?"
"Uh... something with agents?"
[6 months later]
"Why isn't this working?"
This is why FDEs exist. Because the gap between "we have an AI model" and "we've deployed AI that does something useful" is where most companies go to die.
The uncomfortable question:
If OpenAI, Anthropic, and Cohere need specialists to help their own customers deploy AI...what makes you think your eng team can figure it out alone with a Hugging Face tutorial?
Maybe it's time to stop hiring "AI engineers" and start hiring people who can actually ship AI products that solve real problems.
Just a thought. 🤔
