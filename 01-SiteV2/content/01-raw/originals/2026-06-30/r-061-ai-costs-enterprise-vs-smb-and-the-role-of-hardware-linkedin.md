# AI Costs: Enterprise vs SMB and the Role of Hardware - LinkedIn

## clean_text

Your company will probably be buying AI hardware at some point if you're heavy users of it.
Your company will probably be buying AI hardware at some point if you're heavy users of it.
I ran an assessment this morning in Opcode to see what Trust Insights Claude Code usage was for the last 30 days and see what we would have paid if we were on Enterprise instead of Max 20.
It was an eye-popping $7,000 compared to the $200/month Max 20 subscription. For Max 20, that's a bargain. If you're on Enterprise, you'd be paying the full API cost. When people talk about AI costing more than humans it's supposed to replace, this is what they mean - enterprise customers pay rack rate.
Bear in mind, at Trust Insights, three of us are heavy AI users. That's it. $7,000 for three people for a month of usage. Now multiply that times the tens of thousands of users in an enterprise and you can see why [a] Anthropic is chasing enterprise customers as hard as possible along with everyone else, because that's the bank and [b] why people have different perspectives on what AI costs.
If you're a solopreneur or SMB, AI is a bargain. If you're an enterprise, AI is a massive cost.
By comparison, an Asus GX10 AI workstation fully loaded with an NVIDIA GB10 will run you about $5,000 plus the cost of electricity, can run Qwen 3.6 in a variety of flavors, and do about 95% of what Claude Sonnet can do and 90% of what Claude Opus can do, for no more than the cost of electricity - and with no data center costs or fresh water usage, though it might make your office warmer than you're comfortable with. One of these can easily support 5-10 light users and 2-5 heavy users.
If you combine it with good governance and great planning, you could, with today's models, stop using cloud-based AI entirely.
I remember mentioning it to Katie Robbert in passing, and when AI companies inevitably turn the screws on SMB users (because you can't keep selling at massive losses when you're earning $200 on $7,000 of usage/compute, that party has to end sooner or later), that hardware which looks expensive now will look real cheap later.
Open weights models (Qwen, Minimax, Deepseek, Mimo) are 3-6 months behind closed weights models (Claude, OpenAI, Gemini) in terms of capabilities but vastly ahead in terms of costs. Keep your eye on them, and keep an eye on hardware prices. Be ready to pony up once the ultra-low AI cost music stops - and if you want to make sure you have a chair, consider that hardware purchase in this year's CapEx budget.
