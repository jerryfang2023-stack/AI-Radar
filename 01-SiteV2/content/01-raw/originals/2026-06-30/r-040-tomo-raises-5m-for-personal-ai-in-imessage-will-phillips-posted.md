# Tomo Raises $5M for Personal AI in iMessage | Will Phillips posted ...

## clean_text

Consumer technology is one of the hardest categories to build in.
User expectations are high, retention is unforgiving, and distribution is expensive. That’s part of the reason so much of the current AI conversation has focused on enterprise software rather than everyday consumers.
But Justin Quan, Raymond C. and the team at Tomo have spent years pursuing a different vision for AI: personal super-intelligence that lives in iMessage, learns what users care about, understands their goals, and helps keep them accountable.
Tomo has just announced a $5M seed round led by Bain Capital Ventures (BCV), with participation from Conviction, Align Ventures, Basis Set, Pear VC, and Accel. Tomo has gained more than 500,000 users with over 100M messages exchanged through the platform.
I spent a day with the team as they prepared to come out of stealth and expand into personalised software generated around each user’s goals. Justin also took me back to the small San Francisco apartment where the company started, complete with their famous stack of Costco chicken bake boxes.
While much of the AI race has focused on helping companies operate more efficiently, Tomo is exploring a more personal version of the category: AI designed around the goals, ambitions, and habits of the individual using it.
