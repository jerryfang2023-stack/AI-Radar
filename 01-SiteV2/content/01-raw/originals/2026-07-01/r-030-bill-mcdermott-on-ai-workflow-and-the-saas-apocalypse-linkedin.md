# Bill McDermott on AI, Workflow and the SaaS Apocalypse - LinkedIn

## clean_text

AI Thinks.
AI Thinks. Workflow Acts.
Bill McDermott (CEO, ServiceNow) on No Priors with Sarah Guo
I post one executive summary daily of an interview I enjoyed and learnt from. I loved this interview that Sarah Guo did with Bill McDermott, ServiceNow CEO. Great insights for founders and operators.
https://lnkd.in/gVuV_YDu
McDermott takes the "SaaS apocalypse" story apart with arithmetic. Models suggest answers in milliseconds. They do not close cases, carry company context, or pick up the phone when something breaks. The winners sit at the join between AI thinking and workflow action.
1. The 10x Cost Math. Replacing a workflow platform with a language model costs 10x more. GPU bill, tokens, engineers rewriting code, human capital pulled off other work all add up. The model writes new code beautifully. It lacks your data, your context, and someone to call when it gets something wrong.
2. The Trust Asymmetry. People forgive people for mistakes. They never forgive software for them. Models are probabilistic. Enterprises run on deterministic SLAs. "Mostly works" is a non-starter for workflows that run the company.
3. AI Control Tower. ServiceNow's bet: be the fabric connecting hyperscalers, language models, and systems of record. Same move they ran against AWS and Azure last cycle. 85B workflows and 7T transactions run through the platform today. That's the moat.
4. The Ninety-Ten Inversion. 90% of ServiceNow's customer service cases are now handled by agents. 10% involve a human. That ratio is the leading indicator for every services-heavy function: finance, HR, support. Human work that remains is judgment, relationship, and calls software is not allowed to make.
5. Headcount Goes Flat. Net-new hiring drops sharply. 2.2B agents enter the workforce in the next few years. Revenue per employee becomes the metric that matters. The bar for adding a human rises to "an agent cannot do this as well." Expect this on the next board agenda.
6. The Covid Bloat Comeback. Between 2020 and 2024, companies hired through Zoom screens at a scale nobody talks about honestly. AI gives them the cover story to compress those layers and chase revenue per employee. The next two earnings cycles will package a delayed Covid correction as "AI-driven productivity."
7. Department Vendors Are Cooked. Single-function SaaS that touches one department and does not own a system of record sits in the blast radius. Platforms that span departments and own the data are protected.
8. Fully Dressed When the Tide Goes Out. McDermott's leadership rule: don't look for the escape hatch. Choppy water shows who is tough. AI raises the cost of timidity. Leaders who lean into the chaos take the next decade.
Scaling Global Organizations in the Age of AI with ServiceNow Chairman and CEO Bill McDermott
