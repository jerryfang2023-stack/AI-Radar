Ian Smith CA - this is a really important point and one that I think many companies are underestimating right now.
Everyone is racing toward AI adoption and AI agents because of the potential productivity and scaling benefits, but very few organizations are stepping back to ask the governance questions first:
- Who owns the process?
- Who validates the output?
- How are decisions escalated?
- How do you prevent conflicting actions, duplicated work, or unmanaged risk?
Without that structure, AI can very quickly create speed without alignment.
What stood out to me in both the article and your SEG framework is the idea that governance cannot be treated as a separate layer added later. It has to exist in a continuous relationship with strategy and execution or the organization eventually creates noise, redundancy, and operational risk instead of scalable value.
I also think your point about “pace and space” is critical. Smaller and mid-sized companies especially can suffocate themselves by overengineering governance, while others create almost none at all. The balance is what matters.
The companies that win with AI won’t just be the ones that deploy the most tools. They’ll be the ones that create the clearest operating model around how those tools support strategy, execution, accountability, and decision-making.
