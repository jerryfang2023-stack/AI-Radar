An AI that actually does things
Most AI products give you answers. One Agent takes action -- across every app you use, through a single conversation.
One Agent connects to the apps you already use -- Gmail, Calendar, Slack, HubSpot, Jira, QuickBooks, Stripe, and hundreds more -- and does real work inside them. Not summaries. Not suggestions. Actual actions.
Tell it to triage your inbox and draft replies. Ask it to find every open deal over $50K in your CRM and update the forecast. Have it create a Jira ticket from a Slack thread. It reads, writes, and acts across 83,891 tools without you switching tabs.
83,891 skills, one agent
Skills are pre-built actions your agent can take: send an email, search contacts, create an invoice, update a record, file a ticket. One ships with over 80,000 of them across hundreds of apps.
A founder uses their agent to pull weekly revenue from Stripe, post it to Slack, and update a Notion dashboard. A support team routes incoming Zendesk tickets, drafts responses, and escalates blockers to engineering in Linear. A sales rep asks their agent to find every contact at Acme Corp across HubSpot, Gmail, and LinkedIn -- and gets a single answer in seconds.
You control which skills are active. Enable what you need, disable what you don't. Your agent only does what you let it.
Talk to it anywhere
Your agent lives on WhatsApp, Telegram, Slack, or the web. Same agent, same context, same connected apps -- wherever you are.
Start a conversation on your phone during your commute. Pick it up on desktop at work. Ask a follow-up on Slack that afternoon. Your agent remembers what you asked and what it did.
If you're building a product on One, your users get the same flexibility. Deploy agents to any channel from a single API -- no messaging integrations to build or maintain.
It works while you don't
Agents don't just wait for you to ask. They react to events in real time.
A new lead lands in HubSpot -- your agent researches the company, enriches the contact, and drafts an outreach email. A payment hits Stripe -- your agent sends the receipt and updates the books. A support ticket gets escalated in Zendesk -- your agent pings the on-call engineer in Slack with full context.
You define the triggers. One handles webhook subscriptions, delivery, and retries.
See everything it does
Every action your agent takes is logged: what it did, when, in which app, and what happened. A simple timeline shows you exactly what's going on. No black box.
For developers, that means structured logs, tool execution traces, and error surfacing before users report them. For organizations, it means compliance-ready audit trails -- exportable, searchable, and tamper-proof.
Built on an open standard
One Agent is built on MCP (Model Context Protocol), the open standard for connecting AI to tools and data. Your agent works with Cursor, Claude Desktop, and any MCP-compatible client.
That means no lock-in. Your integrations travel with you across AI tools. And if you're building on One, every agent you ship gets MCP compatibility for free -- your users can connect to any client in the ecosystem without extra work on your end.
One agent or 100,000
Use One as a personal assistant with all your apps connected. Or spin up agents programmatically -- one per user, one per workspace, one per department.
A solo founder gets one agent that handles email, CRM, and calendar. A SaaS company provisions an agent per customer with isolated connections and permissions. An enterprise deploys a fleet across sales, support, and ops -- each with the right skills and guardrails -- managed from a single admin console.
Scale from one to a million without changing your architecture.
Under the hood
The agent runtime is built in Rust for speed and reliability. Skill execution runs at under 100ms p95 latency. Webhooks, scheduling, and message routing all happen at the infrastructure layer so your agent logic stays simple.
One works with every major LLM -- OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, Mistral, Llama, and anything else you bring. You pay your AI provider directly. One never charges for tokens. Bring your own model, swap providers any time, no lock-in.
Every integration authenticates through One's managed auth layer. OAuth flows, token refresh, and credential storage are handled automatically. Your agent never touches raw secrets -- it just calls skills and One handles the rest.
Security
All credentials are encrypted at rest and in transit. Each tenant gets full credential isolation -- one user's connections are never accessible to another. Every agent action is audit-logged with structured traces you can export and search.
SOC 2 compliance is in progress. For enterprise deployments, One supports SSO, SCIM provisioning, and granular permission policies so you control exactly what each agent and user can do.
