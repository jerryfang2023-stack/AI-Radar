Open Source Coding Agent Kilo Raises $8M in Seed Funding
Key Points
Kilo Code has raised $8 million in seed funding led by Cota Capital, with participation from Breakers, General Catalyst, Quiet Capital, and Tokyo Black.
Since launching in early 2025, Kilo has reached 750,000 downloads, claimed the #1 spot on OpenRouter, and processes over 6 trillion tokens monthly.
The funds will support Kilo's mission to build an all-in-one agentic platform that eliminates friction from AI-assisted software development.
By Ekaterina Pisareva
December 11, 2025
Credits: TechNews180
Kilo Code, the open source coding agent that has quickly become a favorite among developers, has raised $8 million in seed funding . Cota Capital led the round, joined by Breakers, General Catalyst, Quiet Capital, and Tokyo Black.
The investment comes at a time when Kilo is experiencing explosive growth. Since its launch in early 2025, the platform has amassed over 750,000 downloads and climbed to the #1 position on OpenRouter. The company now processes more than 6 trillion tokens every month.
Why Developers Are Frustrated With AI Coding Tools
The AI coding market is maturing fast. Gartner has been tracking the space with its Magic Quadrant for AI Coding Assistants for two years now, and competition is fierce. Yet many developers feel let down by existing options.
Tools like Cursor and GitHub Copilot have introduced what Kilo calls "AI drag." This is the artificial friction that slows development instead of speeding it up. Downgraded models, rate limiting, confusing pricing structures, and model lock-in have created a new set of headaches for engineering teams.
"At Cota Capital, we back companies building the infrastructure that will define the next era of software, and we see Kilo doing this," said Aditya Singh, General Partner at Cota Capital. "Kilo's vision to build an all-in-one, agentic experience for software developers is what the space needs. Engineering leaders seek simple pricing, high-quality code, and model access to stay ahead of the rapid changes in AI."
Building for Speed, Not Limits
The fresh capital will fuel Kilo's ambitious product roadmap. The company plans to develop smarter multi-agent collaboration capabilities, enterprise-grade tooling for technical leaders, and features designed to accelerate AI adoption across development teams.
Kilo has already been shipping at a rapid pace. In the past month alone, the team has launched parallel agents, one-click deploy, AI code review, cloud agents, and managed indexing. The platform offers access to more than 500 AI models, with new ones added weekly. Kilo works directly with providers including Anthropic, OpenAI, xAI, and Mistral to ensure optimal performance.
For developers, this means flexibility. They can use Kilo in VS Code, JetBrains IDEs, the command line, or in the cloud. Engineering teams get a single platform that handles agentic coding, low-code app building, and deployment. According to the company, technical leaders typically see ROI within their first two weeks of using Kilo.
From Brooklyn Data to Kilo Code
Kilo was co-founded by Scott Breitenother and Sid Sijbrandij. Breitenother previously founded Brooklyn Data, a consultancy that helped companies unlock the value of their data during the rise of cloud data warehouses and the modern data stack. He sold that company and later reconnected with the entrepreneurial world when he met Sijbrandij through a mutual friend.
Sijbrandij brings serious credentials to the table. He co-founded GitLab and currently serves as its Executive Chair. Both founders recognized the potential of AI to make developers dramatically more productive. They also shared frustrations with the tools available at the time.
"Everything we do at Kilo is in pursuit of Kilo Speed," said Scott Breitenother, Co-Founder and CEO of Kilo Code. "We believe this is the new standard for how engineering teams develop and deploy software, and our mission is to help all engineering teams operate at this pace."
What Makes Kilo Different
The company positions itself as the antidote to AI drag. While competitors often lock developers into a limited set of models, Kilo offers more than 500 options. The platform supports everything from frontier models to ultra-low-latency open source alternatives and cost-effective options from emerging labs.
Pricing is another differentiator. Kilo promises transparent costs with no overage fees or confusing fine print. Models never cost more than the list price set by the provider. The company launched a Teams plan in October 2025, followed by an Enterprise plan just two weeks later.
Features like shared modes, credit pooling, managed indexing, and an AI Adoption Dashboard give engineering leaders visibility into how their teams use AI. Data privacy controls and centralized billing round out the enterprise offering.
Strong Investor Backing
The seed round brings together a notable group of investors. Cota Capital, which focuses on infrastructure companies shaping the future of software, led the funding. Breakers, General Catalyst, Quiet Capital, and Tokyo Black participated as well.
This backing reflects confidence in Kilo's approach to the rapidly evolving AI coding space. As new models launch weekly and capabilities expand, Kilo's open-source, model-agnostic architecture is designed to keep developers ahead of the curve rather than waiting for their tools to catch up.

Open Source Coding Agent Kilo Raises $8M in Seed Funding
Key Points
Kilo Code has raised $8 million in seed funding led by Cota Capital, with participation from Breakers, General Catalyst, Quiet Capital, and Tokyo Black.
Since launching in early 2025, Kilo has reached 750,000 downloads, claimed the #1 spot on OpenRouter, and processes over 6 trillion tokens monthly.
The funds will support Kilo's mission to build an all-in-one agentic platform that eliminates friction from AI-assisted software development.
By Ekaterina Pisareva
December 11, 2025
Credits: TechNews180
Kilo Code, the open source coding agent that has quickly become a favorite among developers, has raised $8 million in seed funding . Cota Capital led the round, joined by Breakers, General Catalyst, Quiet Capital, and Tokyo Black.
The investment comes at a time when Kilo is experiencing explosive growth. Since its launch in early 2025, the platform has amassed over 750,000 downloads and climbed to the #1 position on OpenRouter. The company now processes more than 6 trillion tokens every month.
Why Developers Are Frustrated With AI Coding Tools
The AI coding market is maturing fast. Gartner has been tracking the space with its Magic Quadrant for AI Coding Assistants for two years now, and competition is fierce. Yet many developers feel let down by existing options.
Tools like Cursor and GitHub Copilot have introduced what Kilo calls "AI drag." This is the artificial friction that slows development instead of speeding it up. Downgraded models, rate limiting, confusing pricing structures, and model lock-in have created a new set of headaches for engineering teams.
"At Cota Capital, we back companies building the infrastructure that will define the next era of software, and we see Kilo doing this," said Aditya Singh, General Partner at Cota Capital. "Kilo's vision to build an all-in-one, agentic experience for software developers is what the space needs. Engineering leaders seek simple pricing, high-quality code, and model access to stay ahead of the rapid changes in AI."
Building for Speed, Not Limits
The fresh capital will fuel Kilo's ambitious product roadmap. The company plans to develop smarter multi-agent collaboration capabilities, enterprise-grade tooling for technical leaders, and features designed to accelerate AI adoption across development teams.
Kilo has already been shipping at a rapid pace. In the past month alone, the team has launched parallel agents, one-click deploy, AI code review, cloud agents, and managed indexing. The platform offers access to more than 500 AI models, with new ones added weekly. Kilo works directly with providers including Anthropic, OpenAI, xAI, and Mistral to ensure optimal performance.
For developers, this means flexibility. They can use Kilo in VS Code, JetBrains IDEs, the command line, or in the cloud. Engineering teams get a single platform that handles agentic coding, low-code app building, and deployment. According to the company, technical leaders typically see ROI within their first two weeks of using Kilo.
From Brooklyn Data to Kilo Code
Kilo was co-founded by Scott Breitenother and Sid Sijbrandij. Breitenother previously founded Brooklyn Data, a consultancy that helped companies unlock the value of their data during the rise of cloud data warehouses and the modern data stack. He sold that company and later reconnected with the entrepreneurial world when he met Sijbrandij through a mutual friend.
Sijbrandij brings serious credentials to the table. He co-founded GitLab and currently serves as its Executive Chair. Both founders recognized the potential of AI to make developers dramatically more productive. They also shared frustrations with the tools available at the time.
"Everything we do at Kilo is in pursuit of Kilo Speed," said Scott Breitenother, Co-Founder and CEO of Kilo Code. "We believe this is the new standard for how engineering teams develop and deploy software, and our mission is to help all engineering teams operate at this pace."
What Makes Kilo Different
The company positions itself as the antidote to AI drag. While competitors often lock developers into a limited set of models, Kilo offers more than 500 options. The platform supports everything from frontier models to ultra-low-latency open source alternatives and cost-effective options from emerging labs.
Pricing is another differentiator. Kilo promises transparent costs with no overage fees or confusing fine print. Models never cost more than the list price set by the provider. The company launched a Teams plan in October 2025, followed by an Enterprise plan just two weeks later.
Features like shared modes, credit pooling, managed indexing, and an AI Adoption Dashboard give engineering leaders visibility into how their teams use AI. Data privacy controls and centralized billing round out the enterprise offering.
Strong Investor Backing
The seed round brings together a notable group of investors. Cota Capital, which focuses on infrastructure companies shaping the future of software, led the funding. Breakers, General Catalyst, Quiet Capital, and Tokyo Black participated as well.
This backing reflects confidence in Kilo's approach to the rapidly evolving AI coding space. As new models launch weekly and capabilities expand, Kilo's open-source, model-agnostic architecture is designed to keep developers ahead of the curve rather than waiting for their tools to catch up.
