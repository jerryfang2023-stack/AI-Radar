# Andrew Ng on AI's Impact on Startups | Andy Hock posted on the topic

## clean_text

Building startups in the age of AI isn’t about “adding AI.”
Building startups in the age of AI isn’t about “adding AI.”
Here are my top 4 takeaways from the conversation I had in Davos with Andrew Ng, and others at the AI House Davos:
The overarching principle:
2026 is about what AI makes possible.
That was the real theme of a Davos AI House conversation I had with Andrew Ng, Laura Modiano (OpenAI), Alex Ilic (ETH AI Center), and Nicole Büttner Buttner (Merantix Momentum).
A few takeaways that stuck with me:
1. The age of agentic AI is here. 🤓
As Andrew Ng put it, we’re moving toward AI that can complete end-to-end workflows - not just assist with individual tasks. That shift fundamentally changes how software is built and who can build it.
2. Compute is no longer just infrastructure, it's also a product lever. 🚀
In an agentic world, fast inference doesn’t just improve existing experiences. It unlocks entirely new ones that weren’t feasible before. Speed changes what you can ship.
3. Partnerships matter more than ever. 🤝
Laura Modiano highlighted how OpenAI’s models - and partnerships like OpenAI + Cerebras - can act as a real springboard for startups, accelerating both product development and go-to-market.
4. Talent pipelines are incredibly important. 🎓
Alex Ilic described the active role that institutions like ETH Zürich and the ETH AI Center are taking to equip the next generation of builders who will define what AI becomes in the real world. That pipeline is essential for innovation.
From the Cerebras side, my perspective was simple:
- Fast, specialized compute accelerates iteration, learning, and time-to-market
- Inference speed is becoming a differentiator, not a nice-to-have
- Teams win when they pair the right technology with investors and partners who understand the capital and time horizons of deep-tech companies
The builders who win in this era won't be slapping AI onto existing processes, they’ll be using a combination of models, compute, and talent to create something genuinely new.
