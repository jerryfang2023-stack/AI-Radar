# Microsoft adopts AMD Helios AI platform for Azure expansion ...

## clean_text

Microsoft adopts AMD Helios AI platform for Azure expansion
News
July 21, 2026
By
Asma Adhimi
Processors
AMD
AI
Microsoft Azure
Microsoft is significantly expanding its use of AMD technology across Azure, with plans to deploy AMD’s next-generation Helios AI platform alongside new EPYC processor -based virtual machines and broader networking infrastructure upgrades.
The move strengthens the long-term partnership between the two companies as demand for AI infrastructure continues to grow, with Microsoft positioning AMD hardware across AI inference, cloud computing and networking.
For eeNews Europe readers, the announcement highlights how hyperscale cloud providers are increasingly relying on integrated AI infrastructure rather than standalone accelerators. It also signals broader opportunities for European semiconductor, software and cloud developers building AI applications on Azure.
Helios targets large-scale AI inference
At the heart of the expanded collaboration is Microsoft’s deployment of the AMD Helios Rackscale Solution to support frontier AI model inference for Microsoft’s own services, Azure AI offerings and customer workloads.
Helios combines AMD Instinct MI455X GPUs, 6th Gen AMD EPYC “Venice” CPUs, Pensando networking technology and the ROCm software stack into a rack-scale platform designed for AI training and inference. AMD says it will begin shipping Helios systems to customers, including Microsoft, during the second half of 2026.
The deployment gives Azure customers access to AMD-powered infrastructure for both developing large-scale foundation models and running production AI applications through Azure Foundry Managed Compute.
“AMD and Microsoft have spent years building high-performance infrastructure together, and today we’re extending that partnership across the full stack of AMD AI solutions on Azure,” said Dr. Lisa Su, Chair and CEO, AMD. “Microsoft’s new AMD deployments mark an important milestone as we deliver leadership compute solutions to Azure customers and scale the next generation of AI infrastructure together.”
Microsoft also emphasized the growing need for flexible AI computing platforms.
“Customers are looking for AI infrastructure that is optimized for a wide range of workloads, from training and inference to data preparation, search, and reinforcement learning,” said Satya Nadella, Chairman and CEO, Microsoft. “Through our collaboration with AMD, we are expanding the Azure infrastructure portfolio with AMD Helios to give customers the performance, scale and choice they need to build and run the next generation of AI applications.”
New Azure VMs and networking upgrades
Alongside the AI platform rollout, Microsoft will introduce two new Azure virtual machine families based on 6th Gen AMD EPYC “Venice” processors. The Azure HDv2 series is aimed at agentic AI and data pipeline workloads, while Azure HXv2 targets semiconductor design applications, further expanding Azure’s AMD CPU portfolio.
The partnership also extends into cloud networking. Microsoft is increasing its deployment of AMD Pensando data processing units (DPUs) and integrating AMD silicon with Azure Boost technology to improve networking performance, efficiency and connection processing across Azure infrastructure.
The latest announcements demonstrate how cloud providers are investing in tightly integrated compute, networking and software platforms to meet rapidly growing AI demand. For AMD, securing a broader role across Microsoft’s AI infrastructure represents another important hyperscale design win as competition in AI data centre hardware continues to intensify.
