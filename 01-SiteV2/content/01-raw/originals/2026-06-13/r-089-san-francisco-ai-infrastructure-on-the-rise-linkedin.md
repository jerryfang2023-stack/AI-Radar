Your AI startup's moat just disappeared. 🪦
Three top VCs landed on the same thesis this week: the foundation model isn't the defensible layer anymore. a16z, Nazaré, and robotwave all wrote it from different angles. The moat is the specialized harness - the encoded expertise, the proprietary data, the workflow built around the actual job.
Want the proof? 👇
Anthropic just put $1.5B into a JV with Blackstone, Goldman, and H&F to embed engineers inside mid-sized businesses.
OpenAI launched DeployCo one week later. $4B from a 19-firm consortium. Bought Tomoro's 150 forward-deployed engineers on day one.
If general intelligence captured the value, the labs wouldn't be spending $5.5B in two weeks to position themselves between their own models and the customer. 🧠
The pitch reframe for founders raising right now:
❌ "We use AI to do X" — this is a baseline, not a positioning
✅ "Our harness handles [regulatory liability / proprietary data / workflow-specific UI]" — this is a moat
If your only moat is a capability gap, you'll get Sherlocked by next quarter. 📉
If your moat compounds while the model improves underneath it? You're fine. 📈

DeepSeek just raised $7B at a $59B valuation. first outside funding round ever.
backers include Tencent, CATL, and founder Liang Wenfeng putting in $20B of his own money. twenty billion. personal check.
this is happening the same week Anthropic filed its S-1 confidentially. two of the biggest AI labs on earth are racing to lock in capital at the same time.
i run a 2-person startup on $200/month in AI tools. we ship daily from Seoul. no funding round, no investors, no billion-dollar war chest.
here's what most people miss about these mega-rounds: every dollar raised is a dollar that needs to be earned back. DeepSeek now owes returns on $59B in expectations. Anthropic is about to owe public markets.
meanwhile the cost of the actual models keeps dropping. DeepSeek open-sourced their reasoning model. Claude gets cheaper every quarter. the weapons these labs are spending billions to build? they hand them to us for $200/month.
the AI arms race is the best subsidy small builders have ever gotten. every time a lab raises billions to compete, they push capabilities up and prices down. two people in Seoul benefit more from that dynamic than a 500-person company trying to justify its headcount.
the best time to build with AI is when the giants are burning cash to outspend each other. that's right now.
