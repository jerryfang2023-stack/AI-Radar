# Why companies are slow to adopt open models - LinkedIn

## clean_text

Today's gap in open model adoption is mostly due to a lack in industry maturity rather than the state of open models.
Today's gap in open model adoption is mostly due to a lack in industry maturity rather than the state of open models. Here's why:
The tooling stack around open models has improved so much over the past few years: fine-tuning a model or running inference is now just a few lines of code, as easy as it was to build a decision tree or linear regression a few years ago.
And open models have become very strong, too! True, frontier models are maybe a few months ahead still on PhD level questions, but on a wide range of use-cases the performance of open and closed models is hard to distinguish.
There are several advantages to developing on open models:
> full control over the models and data
> small, specialized models can be cheaper
> models fine-tuned on in-domain data can still outperform frontier models
So what's keeping many companies from adopting them?
Using API models comes with little upfront cost while learning to use models locally requires still some know-how and many companies invested in the former so they are now lacking talent and skills to:
> have their own infra to train/infer
> developing high-quality datasets
> building meaningful evaluations
> running models efficiently and locally
The good news is that the stack is not very deep, a few engineers/data scientists with the right resources can get up to speed quickly.
Open and API models can co-exist, but depending on just one provider is probably a bad idea. Especially core business applications comapanies will want to keep control over and also build specialized models to keep a competitive advantage in the long term.
