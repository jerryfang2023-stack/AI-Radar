Inference costs dropped 280-fold over the last two years.
Inference costs dropped 280-fold over the last two years. So why are enterprise AI bills still unsustainable?
Recently, I spoke with a number of executives at larger enterprises, all of whom are finding that their inference costs are still too high. The paradox is straightforward: while per-query costs have dropped dramatically, total spending is exploding because usage has outpaced cost reductions. The widespread adoption of agentic AI will surely increase this expense, as systems run continuous inference loops, not just on-demand queries.
One executive I spoke to said he’s evaluating building on-prem inference infrastructure. He wasn’t quite sure when, but that got me searching for answers and led me to a report from Deloitte (link in comments). The research found that when cloud costs exceed 60-70% of the capital cost of equivalent on-prem systems, the economics of inference costs flip.
But it's not just about cost. Data sovereignty, IP protection, and latency requirements for real-time applications—are all important considerations . Bringing AI capabilities to their data, rather than moving sensitive information to external services, matters more as AI moves from experimentation to production operations.
What I find interesting is that the solution isn't binary—cloud or on-prem. (Gone are the days of going “all in” on the cloud?) Rather hybrid architectures seem to be the way forward: public cloud for training and experimentation, on-prem for production inference, edge computing for latency-sensitive decisions (e.g. Autonomous Vehicles).
To the extent this hybrid approach feels inevitable, what’s less certain is its impact on AI-software providers. Permissioning and processing of customer data will likely shift. Pricing models assume cloud-based consumption will need to account for hybrid deployment. But will providers offer on-prem solutions as subscriptions? Will adoption of this architecture paradigm be sudden or a slow uptake? A lot is still TBD. What do you think?
