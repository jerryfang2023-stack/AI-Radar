# Jessica Wu's AI Startup Sola Raises $21M with Y Combinator

## clean_text

Jessica Wu left her hedge fund job and raised $21M for her AI startup with Y Combinator.
Jessica Wu left her hedge fund job and raised $21M for her AI startup with Y Combinator.
She’s the co-founder and CEO of Sola, a platform that helps businesses automate their most critical workflows using AI.
Here's her story:
1. She studied Math and CS at MIT
It put her in rooms full of people building things that didn't exist yet. That's also where she met Neil Deshmukh - her future co-founder.
2. She explored venture capital first
After MIT, she spent time in VC learning how the best companies were built.
It gave her a bird's eye view of what worked and what didn't.
3. She became the youngest quant researcher at a major hedge fund
Not her final destination, but an important one. Finance taught her to be objective, calculate odds, and stay rational under pressure.
4. She spotted the problem nobody was fixing
Massive companies copying data, clicking through broken software, wasting hours on simple tasks. That frustration became the seed of Sola.
5. She walked into YC without a finished product
They had a problem, not a product. YC advised: go sell it anyway. They told their first customer it didn't exist yet, and promised it would. They agreed.
6. They made customer obsession their edge
Weekly calls. Fast shipping. That's how a tiny startup earns the trust of decades-old companies. Most of their customers still come through word of mouth.
And today, they’ve raised $21M+ in funding from a16z, Conviction, and Y Combinator.
Jessica had no product, no customers, and no guarantee it would work. She just understood the problem better than anyone else - and kept showing up.
What do you think of her journey?
