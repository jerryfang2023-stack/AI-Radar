# Enterprise AI moves beyond model access to workflow redesign and ...

## clean_text

OpenAI and Anthropic both launching enterprise AI deployment companies is a clear signal: the model companies are not just selling APIs anymore.
OpenAI and Anthropic both launching enterprise AI deployment companies is a clear signal: the model companies are not just selling APIs anymore. They are moving into deployment, workflow redesign, and the services work that consulting firms and SIs have historically owned.
Enterprise AI is no longer about access to the best model. Everyone has access to powerful models now. The harder question is who can actually turn that intelligence into production systems that work inside a real enterprise.
That is where buyers are getting sharper.
They are no longer buying every “AI native” claim. Traditional SIs cannot run the same old services playbook, add agents to the pitch deck, and expect the market to believe they have suddenly become AI native.
It is important to know what models are good at, where they fail, how to evaluate them, how to design agentic workflows, how to build safeguards, and how to make the system reliable enough for real business use.
Enterprise buyers want a rare combination: deep AI experience, real understanding of their business, and the ability to make AI work on top of existing systems, workflows, permissions, exceptions, and organizational memory.
The winners will not be the firms that simply “use AI.” They will be the firms that can combine real AI depth with real operational understanding.
We are spending a lot of time with enterprise teams thinking through what AI agents should look like on top of their existing software systems. Especially their front office workflows around sales, service, and customer operations.
If you are thinking through this inside your own organization, I’d be excited to talk.
