# Why Forward Deployed Engineers Are the Rage | Carly Rector

## clean_text

This article from the Information contains possibly the best simple description of the "Forward-Deployed Engineer" role I've seen so far, though it still has flaws.
This article from the Information contains possibly the best simple description of the "Forward-Deployed Engineer" role I've seen so far, though it still has flaws.
They describe the role as follows:
"The FDE boom reflects a central challenge AI providers are facing even as their sales rise dramatically. While most large companies are using some form of AI, many lack the institutional knowledge to develop complex AI agents that tap data from different business units, including sales and marketing, finance and human resources, according to consultants who work with these firms. [...] To help companies bridge that gap, FDEs often serve as part software engineer, part business consultant and part product manager."
"Part software engineer, part business consultant and part product manager" is the closest breakdown to how I think about the role, and also captures why it's a difficult role to hire for. It's extremely useful to have a perspective that understands both a customer problem/business opportunity *and* the shape of the technical systems available to solve the problem, but traditional roles don't really incentivize developing that breadth of perspective. I struggled with this even at Amazon, where engineers have more product "ownership" than most companies.
I still think the description of the need is a bit narrow, however.
It's not just about the complexity of the AI implementation, or the need to "tap data" from different business units. It starts more fundamentally than that, with identifying the problems that exist that AI can effectively solve, both internal to the organization and in the products they offer.
And, more than lacking institutional knowledge, organizations can often get tunnel vision based on existing ways of doing things, or simply not have a concept of the capabilities of AI and the ways it can be applied. Those things are still being figured out in real time, and part of the advantage of a forward-deployed engineer is being able to synthesize learnings across various deployments.
