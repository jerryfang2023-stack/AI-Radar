I think it’s great that AI use is becoming more common and encouraged in many workplaces. Because of this, one of the biggest misconceptions about AI adoption is that success comes from choosing the best model. It tends to be the organizations seeing meaningful results are usually the ones that rethink the workflow first.
A poorly designed process doesn’t magically become efficient because AI is added to it. In many cases, it just allows the organization to make the same mistakes faster. This article highlights a point that I feel organizations need to embrace: AI ROI comes from full scale workflow transformation, not from deploying another flashy tool and just hoping for the best.
It doesn’t matter if it’s acquisition, project management, customer service, or software development, the real challenge isn’t the model. It’s understanding the process, identifying bottlenecks, and redesigning how the work gets done. Yes, the technology definitely matters, but workflow matters more.
