This post from Aaron Levie is one of the most thoughtful breakdowns I’ve seen of what it’ll really take to make AI agents work at scale inside enterprises.
What stands out to me most is how foundational first-party data is across almost every challenge listed — from agent interoperability to compliance to execution. Agents can’t act intelligently if they don’t have access to the right data, in the right format, with the right controls in place!
But the data also has to move:
- Across systems
- With strong permissioning
- In formats agents can actually use
- And with governance baked in from the start
It’s easy to get caught up in the excitement around agent orchestration, LLMs, and UX layers — but without solving the data layer, nothing else scales responsibly.
We’re still early, but I think the real unlock will come from teams that treat data infrastructure as strategic, not just technical plumbing. That’s what will separate prototypes from production. Thoughts?
