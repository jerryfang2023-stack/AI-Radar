## clean_text

The hottest AI companies of 2026 are built around the Forward Deployed Engineer.
The hottest AI companies of 2026 are built around the Forward Deployed Engineer.
I mapped 26 companies actively running FDE-native motions across 6 sectors.
Every name has a live job posting or on-record commentary to back it up.
The pattern is too consistent to ignore:
1/ Defense was the leading indicator.
⮑ Helsing, Vannevar Labs, Saronic, Shield AI are all hiring Cleared FDEs by the dozens. Classified deployments leave no other option.
2/ Frontier model labs have quietly become FDE shops.
⮑ OpenAI, Anthropic, Cohere are all expanding forward-deployed teams. Building the model is not enough. Enterprises need engineers who fine-tune it on-site.
3/ Legal AI is essentially a services business with software margins.
⮑ Harvey runs a Founding Forward Deployed Engineer program. The deployment model looks more McKinsey than SaaS.
4/ Enterprise agents only reach 90%+ accuracy through deep customer-side integration.
⮑ Sierra publicly described its model as Palantir's forward-deployed ethos applied to agents. Decagon, Glean, Cresta are all hiring Deployment Strategists.
5/ Coding agents are the same story.
⮑ Cognition, Cursor, Poolside all require humans in the customer's repo for real enterprise deployments.
6/ Even AI-native SaaS plays this way now.
⮑ Rippling and Ramp use FDEs to handle complex enterprise migrations.
The thesis:
AI eats services and turns them into software with deployment models that still require humans on the ground.
VCs used to penalize services revenue.
Now they fund it because in AI, the FDE motion is the moat.
At Landbase we learned this firsthand.
We deployed GTM engineers and data scientists directly with our enterprise clients.
It helped us win deals and become a genuine advisor on how customers get the most out of our agents, tools, and data.
The FDE motion is core to how we build and deliver.
