# Claude’s voice mode is now available for Opus and Sonnet

## clean_text

AI
News
Anthropic
Claude’s voice mode is now available for Opus and Sonnet
Anthropic is also granting voice mode access to your apps like Gmail and Slack.
Anthropic is also granting voice mode access to your apps like Gmail and Slack.
by Terrence O'Brien
Jul 23, 2026, 7:00 PM UTC
Image: Anthropic
Terrence O'Brien is the Verge’s weekend editor. He’s covered the tech industry for over 18 years and knows a thing or two about synths.
Until now, voice mode has only been available on Claude Haiku , Anthropic’s faster but less powerful model. Now the company is making its Opus and Sonnet models available in voice mode , and extending its reach into apps like Gmail, Slack, and Canva.
When Anthropic launched voice mode last year, it was primarily focused on delivering answers to quick questions with minimal delay. But in a blog post, the company said people immediately started using voice mode for far more than casual queries. They were using it to work “through real business problems,” which Haiku was not really designed for. That model “kept conversations quick, but not always deep.”
Sonnet and Opus are deeper models that the company says are “designed for hard problem-solving.” They can deliver more complex responses and analysis, and then take action on your behalf. For instance, turn a conversation you have with it into a one-page pitch, or shift around your calendar appointments if your train is running late.
Users can shift back and forth between text and voice mode mid-conversation, as well as change models. So if a quick chat with Haiku sparks an idea, you can explore it further by seamlessly switching to Opus. Anthropic is also expanding voice mode to several new languages. Until now, languages other than English were only available in beta, but now voice mode is available in French, German, Spanish, Hindi, Indonesian, Italian, Japanese, Korean, and Portuguese.
Correction July 23rd: This story originally stated that voice mode launched in 2026, it launched in 2025.
