# Invert Procurement AI Thinking from Automation to Augment and ...

## clean_text

A pattern I keep noticing in procurement AI conversations and business case reviews: Most still lead with automation: replace this step, remove that role, take the FTE impact to the CFO.
A pattern I keep noticing in procurement AI conversations and business case reviews: Most still lead with automation: replace this step, remove that role, take the FTE impact to the CFO. It's the easiest ROI to quantify, so it's the one that usually makes the deck.
We must invert that thinking.
The most successful projects I've been involved with show a clear pattern. They rebalance the framework. Less emphasis on what we can take out of the process, more on how we augment talent and compress time.
Some examples from recent engagements:
💪 *Augment*
Same people, same hours, supercharged to drive improved outcomes.
- Category managers walking into negotiations with a wider range of actionable analysis that used to take days (or weeks) to find.
- Contract and supplier managers catching and mitigating risks that was previously buried too deep in documents and disconnected from what was moving in the wider market.
- Sourcing leads freed from the manual stakeholder communication that used to crowd out the broader inputs capable of actually improving the outcome.
This next level of work wasn't getting done before. AI augmented the role, and the output measurably changed.
🏃‍➡️ *Compress*
Same people, same outcomes, with reduced cycle times.
- Sourcing events that used to consume six weeks running end to end now done in ten days.
- RFP response evaluations that would have taken a sourcing manager four days done in an afternoon.
- Supplier onboarding collapsed from a month to under a week, because they now happen more intelligently.
The work was always getting done. It just wasn't getting done as quickly as it now can be, freeing up resource to manage more spend/suppliers, scaling outcomes.
Then you can get to
🤖 *Automate*
Rules-based work lifted out of the human path.
Across the 42 enterprise business cases I've reviewed, almost all led with automation and FTE impact. Few were built primarily around the compounding value of capacity recovered, or the strategic value of decisions made faster and better. Hands down, augment and compress consistently delivered the larger share of realised ROI in the work I have done or seen, and yet was under represented in client business cases when first shared with me. I then poked, really hard 😁
Full disclose: many come back to me and suggest that augment and compress are just rehashes of automation shaped in different forms and the three areas are not mutually exclusive. Yes! But that’s not the key point… augment and compress before automate forces leaders to ask different questions, in the right order and rethink the types of outcomes to drive with AI. Then the real magic happens with increased success rates and ROI.
