700 customers.
700 customers. 30% adoption. $150 per month. $378,000 of new ARR on Voice AI add-on. That's the back-of-envelope math I ran after a call with a laundromat software founder this week.
Voice AI is not an AI business. It is a feature any vertical SaaS founder can ship.
This is the easiest distribution play in voice AI right now. Almost no vertical SaaS founder has done the math.
1. He sells software to 700 laundromat owners. POS, online ordering, pickup and delivery routing. They pay him every month. He is their default vendor for anything new he ships.
2. The voice AI use case is dead simple. Phone rings while staff is folding clothes. Caller wants hours, pricing, service area, order status. Owner already has every answer sitting in his platform. Upload a knowledge base, 80 to 90% of calls handled without pulling staff off the floor. Owners stop losing orders to missed calls.
3. None of those laundromats will evaluate ten voice AI tools themselves. One location, two employees, no tech team, no time. They won't.
4. He doesn't need a single new customer. One email to his existing list books 100 demos.
5. Same math works for the dental SaaS founder, the HVAC platform, the legal practice tool, the pet groomer scheduler, the pool service ERP. Any vertical SaaS with 100s or 1,000s of captive customers.
For a vertical SaaS founder with a captive customer base, voice AI is the easiest upsell layer you will ever ship. You have the customers. You have the trust. You have the recurring billing already in place. Most of you have not done the math.
The moat is not the agent. Not the platform. The customer who has been paying you for 3 years and will buy whatever you ship next.
In 2026, the voice AI business that wins isn't the one with the best agent.
It is the one that already owns the customers.
If you run vertical SaaS and just did the same math, the only question is whether you ship before someone else does it for your customers.
