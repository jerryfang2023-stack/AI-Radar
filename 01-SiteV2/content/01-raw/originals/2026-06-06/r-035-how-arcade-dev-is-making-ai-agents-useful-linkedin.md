# How Arcade.dev is making AI agents useful - LinkedIn

## clean_text

For this week’s column, I traveled to South San Francisco, where a startup is tackling the last obstacle standing in the way of truly useful AI agents that can perform complex, multipart tasks.
For this week’s column, I traveled to South San Francisco, where a startup is tackling the last obstacle standing in the way of truly useful AI agents that can perform complex, multipart tasks. And it’s a big one — creating the authorization and permission structures that will allow agents to efficiently and safely access the apps, APIs, websites and more that we rely on. Alex Salazar and Sam Partee co-founded Arcade.dev to solve that problem. As that obstacle is overcome, the use of AI agents has the potential to scale during the next two years much as apps did in 2008, changing the way things get done. Anthropic protocols have advancd this AI tooling capability, too. Adoption of AI agents has already started, as companies like Shortwave use Arcade to connect its inbox agent to other apps. The question now is how far and how fast agents can scale. That’s less a matter of creating ever smarter models — the ones we have now are good enough for many tasks- and building up the other systems that support the LLMs at the heart of AI agents. Massive engineering efforts are focused on that problem right now.
The Wall Street Journal WSJ Leadership Institute Vinny Steves Tanya Dua
