We held our annual Media Day today, and I wanted to share a quick note on why we're focused on becoming the leading Enterprise AI Orchestrator.

It helped them pick the right database for the right use case — relational for transactions, key-value for scale, graph for relationships, time-series for telemetry. Trying to force one engine to do everything is how you end up with expensive workarounds and brittle systems.
A decade later, the same lesson is playing out again — this time in AI. The AI landscape is changing every week. LLM one-upmanship, new agent frameworks, new AI SaaS apps, new infrastructure choices. Your CEO's view of AI is not your software developer's view. A customer-service agent, a code-review agent, and a financial-compliance agent have very different business requirements. They'll need different models, different data, different guardrails, and often different infrastructure.
This is what the Multi-Agent Ecosystem actually looks like in practice. Powerful, but genuinely complex. The companies that win in this environment will be the ones who learn how to choose — app by app, model by model, workload by workload — and then orchestrate it all coherently. That is hard to do alone. It requires breadth of expertise across CSPs, AI applications, data platforms, security, and enterprise applications, and depth in the industries where the work actually gets done.
