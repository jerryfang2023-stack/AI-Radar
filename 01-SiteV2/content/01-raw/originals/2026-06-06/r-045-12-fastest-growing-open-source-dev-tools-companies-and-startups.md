1. Anysphere (Cursor) — AI-Native Code Editor Leader
What They Do:
Anysphere develops Cursor, an AI-native code editor built on a fork of VS Code but optimized specifically for AI collaboration. Unlike traditional IDEs with AI extensions, Cursor is designed from the ground up for AI-powered development, offering features like multi-file editing with natural language commands, tab completion with AI context awareness, and support for multiple AI models including GPT-4 and Claude.
Why They're Important:
Anysphere represents the shift from AI-as-extension to AI-native development environments, enabling developers to work with entire codebases through natural language. The platform addresses data privacy concerns by offering local AI processing options, and viral adoption among engineering teams has made it a new standard in AI coding. Market recognition includes achieving a $29.3B valuation and securing investments from tier-one firms including Andreessen Horowitz, Thrive Capital, Google, and NVIDIA.

2. Chainguard — Software Supply Chain Security Leader
What They Do:
Chainguard provides enterprise-grade supply chain security solutions built on open source foundations. The company created Sigstore, the open standard for software signing, and offers low-to-zero CVE container images that address critical vulnerabilities. Their platform enables organizations to verify software provenance and secure the entire software delivery pipeline.
Why They're Important:
Chainguard addresses critical post-SolarWinds/Log4j supply chain security vulnerabilities, with Sigstore becoming the industry default for software signing. The enterprise focus ensures compliance with strict security requirements, while the open source foundation provides transparency and community trust.
