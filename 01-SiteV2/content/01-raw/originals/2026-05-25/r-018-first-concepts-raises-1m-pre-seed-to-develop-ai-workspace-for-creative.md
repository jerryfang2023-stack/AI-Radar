First Concepts raises $1M pre-seed to develop AI workspace for creative teams
March 17, 2026
Share now
Read this article in:
© First Concepts
First Concepts, a startup developing an AI-powered workspace for creative professionals, has raised $1 million in pre-seed funding less than a year after launching.
The round was led by Arāya Ventures with participation from Antler and several industry figures, including Jez Jowett, Nathan McDonald, Sean Williams, Sam Winward, and Hugo Rodger Brown. The company is focused on improving how creative teams manage early-stage project development by introducing structured context and consistency across workflows.
What The Company Does
First Concepts develops an AI-native workspace designed to support creative teams during the early stages of project development, particularly in environments such as agencies where multiple pitches are managed simultaneously.
Creative workflows often rely on a combination of disconnected tools, which can lead to fragmented context and inefficiencies when teams need to revisit or rebuild ideas. First Concepts addresses this by introducing an AI layer that integrates with existing tools while maintaining a shared understanding of project context.
The platform coordinates different AI models through a centralized system that incorporates what the company describes as “taste” and contextual awareness. This allows teams to maintain consistency in creative direction, brand identity, and decision-making throughout the development process.
The product is structured around three core components. A Creative DNA engine captures and learns individual and brand-specific preferences. A unified interface treats context as the central reference point for all creative work. A tool-agnostic infrastructure connects various creative applications while ensuring coherence across outputs.
By organizing context as a persistent asset rather than a temporary input, the platform aims to reduce duplication of work and improve the efficiency of moving from initial brief to final concept.
Advertisement
Market Context / Industry Background
Creative industries, particularly advertising and design agencies, operate in high-pressure environments where multiple pitches and projects are developed in parallel. These workflows often require rapid ideation while maintaining alignment with brand guidelines and client expectations.
However, much of the creative process remains fragmented across tools such as design platforms, presentation software, and communication systems. This fragmentation can result in lost context, repeated work, and inconsistencies in output.
The emergence of generative AI has introduced new opportunities for accelerating creative production, but it has also added complexity in managing outputs across multiple tools and models. As a result, there is growing interest in platforms that can unify these systems while preserving creative intent and consistency.
First Concepts is part of a broader category of AI-native productivity tools that aim to restructure workflows by embedding context and decision-making logic directly into the platform layer.
Founder / Investor Commentary
The company was founded by Conor Hoey, Polina Sali, and Marin Godechot, who bring backgrounds in design, artificial intelligence, and product development.
Hoey described the company’s focus as addressing the way creative context is handled within teams.
“First Concepts has a simple mission to transform creative context from something fragile and ephemeral into a structured, compounding asset,” he said. “We are building an operating system that understands the value of creative thinking and taste.”
He added that the goal is to create a system that supports how creative professionals work, rather than forcing them into rigid workflows, with the aim of improving both efficiency and output quality.
Growth Plans / Use Of Funds
The $1 million pre-seed funding will be used to further develop the platform’s AI capabilities and expand its product offering. The company plans to refine its workspace to better support collaborative creative processes and improve integration with existing tools used by agencies and design teams.
Investment will also support early user acquisition and product iteration as the company continues to validate its approach within the creative industry.
By focusing on early-stage workflows, First Concepts aims to position itself as a foundational layer for creative project development, with potential expansion into broader areas of the creative process over time.
About First Concepts
First Concepts is an AI-powered workspace designed for creative teams, focused on managing context and maintaining consistency across projects. Founded in London, the company builds tools that integrate with existing creative platforms while embedding contextual awareness and creative preferences into workflows. Its platform enables teams to move from initial brief to concept more efficiently while preserving coherence across outputs.
