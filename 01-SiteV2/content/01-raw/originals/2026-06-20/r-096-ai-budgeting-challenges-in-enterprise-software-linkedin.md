# AI Budgeting Challenges in Enterprise Software - LinkedIn

## clean_text

Across over a 100 Enterprise AI deals this past year, one reasonably frequent topic that I have seen emerge, especially from veteran tech sourcers, is how to account for budgeting and chargebacks.
Across over a 100 Enterprise AI deals this past year, one reasonably frequent topic that I have seen emerge, especially from veteran tech sourcers, is how to account for budgeting and chargebacks. AI is breaking how enterprise software is budgeted.
Most companies are still organized around vendor and departmental spend. CRM budgets, Analytics Budgets, ITSM, ERP, Data, etc.
Clean lines. Clear owners.
But AI doesn’t fit cleanly into any of those. If one AI layer pulls from CRM, triggers workflows, analyzes data and generates outputs across teams, who owns that spend? Everyone wants the outcome. No one wants the bill.
And it gets messier when value spans multiple teams, consumption is variable and not really budgetable. And ROI is hard to isolate to one function.
This isn’t just a pricing problem for vendors. It’s an operating model problem for buyers and internal budget politics. And as I have learned, an entirely new set of needed objection handling approaches when you - or your partners, do an Enterprise AI sale. None of which were in the old playbooks.
