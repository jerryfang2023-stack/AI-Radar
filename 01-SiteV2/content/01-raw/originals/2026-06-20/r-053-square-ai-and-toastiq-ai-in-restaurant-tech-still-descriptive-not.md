# Square AI and ToastIQ: AI in restaurant tech, still descriptive, not ...

## clean_text

Yesterday I tried Square’s new AI assistant for the first time.
Yesterday I tried Square’s new AI assistant for the first time. Here are my thoughts:
Square AI is a conversational layer that lets business owners ask questions about sales, staff, or operations instead of digging through dashboards. It can also return visuals such as pie charts, bar graphs, and tables, and you can pin those outputs to your dashboard where they auto update. In practice, it works as both an analytics assistant and an interactive dashboard builder.
That part was cool.
I’ve also looked at what Toast is doing with AI through their ToastIQ engine. It is more feature driven than conversational: menu upsell prompts, Digital Chits that surface guest context for servers, Shift at a Glance for managers, and AI assisted marketing campaigns. Valuable, yes, but not yet the kind of open ended, prescriptive agent that can tell you what to do next across domains.
I’m seeing a theme here. We are still stuck in the “descriptive” phase and haven’t yet crossed into “prescriptive.” And I don’t think we get there until restaurant tech becomes far less fragmented. To be prescriptive, you need a holistic 360-degree view of the business, sales, labor, inventory, reservations, marketing, guest data, all living together, clean and contextualized. The challenge is that today’s platforms are limited to the data they generate themselves, which means the insights they deliver will always be partial, always “descriptive.”
And that’s the limitation I keep coming back to. Fragmented systems make prescriptive intelligence difficult. Consolidated, cross domain context is the missing piece.
So my takeaway is this: these AI assistants are not replacing reporting yet. They are reframing how we interact with it. The breakthrough will come when platforms can unify context deeply enough for AI to move beyond surfacing insights into prescribing actions.
I’m still excited by these advancements, it shows where we may go. But as someone who is a data expert, doesn’t really float my boat, not yet.
