# The Rise of the Forward Deployment Engineer: A Message to My ...

## clean_text

As we move toward the end of the fall semester and you begin preparing for your next internship or that first job after graduation in the spring, I can already see the familiar mix of excitement and anxiety on your faces. You’re eager to build something meaningful, launch your career, and land that dream role. But at the same time, you’re reading headlines about layoffs, watching the tech market shift, and hearing constant chatter about AI taking jobs.
Let me tell you something honestly, as someone who's been in this industry for 25 years and has seen three major platform shifts already.
Yes, some jobs will go away. But new ones are emerging just as fast. And the people who understand both technology and the messy reality of real customers will be in higher demand than ever.
One of the fastest-growing roles I want you to pay attention to is this:
The Forward Deployment Engineer (FDE)
(also called Forward Deployed Software Engineer, Agent Deployment Engineer, Applied AI Engineer)
If you haven’t heard this title yet, you will soon.
FDEs sit at the intersection of engineering, product, and field teams. They take new AI systems, agents, micro services, or platforms and deploy them directly into real customer workflows. These engineers translate ambiguous business problems into working solutions and make AI useful in the real world.
Think of them as:
part engineer
part problem-solver
part product thinker
part on-the-ground strategist
And here’s the truth: AI isn’t replacing this role. AI is making this role essential.
Why This Role Matters in the AI-First World
Most companies are not struggling with “building AI models.” They’re struggling with:
connecting AI to legacy(legendary) systems
making it trustworthy
integrating it with real data and real workflows
deploying it in messy environments with multiple constraints
operationalizing agents across security, compliance, and governance
showing business value in weeks, not years
That’s where FDEs shine.
They turn slides into systems. They turn prototypes into products. They turn “AI hype” into measurable value.
And every major company is hiring for this.
What FDEs Actually Do Every Day
An FDE might:
Instrument a microservices stack to support an AI workflow
Build a small RAG pipeline and deploy it into an existing support system
Integrate an AI agent with an API that was never built for AI
Debug why an agent refuses to call the right endpoint
Help a customer define success metrics for AI automation
Customize an AI solution for a domain-specific workflow (HR, finance, supply chain)
Translate between business users and engineering teams
Identify where automation makes sense, and where it doesn’t
Deploy secure AI systems within strict governance frameworks
Run real-world experiments and track ROI
This role requires zero ego, a ton of creativity, and a willingness to learn constantly.
A Personal Note to You as Your Professor
The job market looks turbulent because it is . But turbulent markets create openings for people who are adaptable, curious, and unafraid to learn new things.
If you’ve done well in this class, you already have the DNA for these roles:
You understand distributed systems.
You’ve built micro services.
You know cloud architectures.
You've integrated APIs and worked with data systems.
You've touched AI pipelines and agentic workflows.
You've debugged problems with incomplete information.
These are exactly the skills an FDE uses every day.
Practical Tips to Prepare Yourself (Starting Now)
These are not generic career suggestions. These are laser-focused moves that increase your chances of success in the next 12 months:
1. Learn how to deploy end-to-end AI workflows
Take a dataset → vectorize → store in vector DB → build a RAG pipeline → wrap it as an API → observe it → secure it.
A few tutorials:
5 Minute RAG with Qdrant and DeepSeek
The Ultimate Guide to Vector DB and RAG Pipeline
If you can do this, you're instantly in the top 15 percent of new grads.
2. Build comfort with ambiguity
FDEs don't ask “what exactly should I build?” They ask “what problem are we solving?”
Show you can handle open-ended projects.
A few resources:
Stanford d school: Navigating Ambiguity
MIT "How to Speak" by Patrick Winston

3. Strengthen your “business sense”
Learn how to ask:
“What does success look like?”
“Who is the user?”
“What is the ROI?”
Technical excellence plus business context is an unbeatable combination.
A few resources and guides:
“What Is Product Sense? Complete Guide (2025)” by Parallel HQ
“How to Calculate ROI to Justify a Project” (HBS Online Blog)
4. Get hands-on with at least one agentic framework (OpenAI, WatsonX Orchestrate, LangChain, Open-WebUI, AgentForce)
Don’t just read docs. Build something small and deploy it.
A few tutorials:
Building Agents with OpenAI
Building an AI Agent with Watsonx Orchestrate ADK
5. Practice integrating AI into existing systems
Companies do not want “AI that lives in a notebook.” They want AI inside CRMs, support systems, HR tools, ERPs, and internal APIs.
Show you understand integration patterns.
A couple of tutorials:
Building an AI bot for your team slack
Zapier and OpenAI ChatGPT Integration
6. Document everything you build
FDEs write:
architecture diagrams
integration notes
trade-off decisions
API flows
dashboards
Most new grads ignore documentation. Don’t be one of them. Use some of these:
LLMs for creating Software Architecture Diagrams
IBM Bob - AI coding agent
7. Put one strong applied-AI project on your resume
Make it something where AI actually did useful work, not just a toy demo.
Recruiters love:
support automation
agent-based task orchestration
observability agents
workflow automation
industry-specific AI tools (healthcare, retail, finance, manufacturing)
A Few Encouraging Words Before You Graduate
You are entering a market that looks scary because technology is shifting fast.
But here’s the part nobody says out loud:
The students who adapt quickly are about to have the most exciting, impactful careers of the next decade.
AI will take some jobs. That’s real.
But AI will amplify the people who:
think critically
learn continuously
understand architecture
enjoy solving real-world problems
can integrate AI into systems that actually matter
That's you.
And if you choose to lean into this new role, the FDE, the applied AI engineer, the agent deployment specialist, you’re not just getting a job.
You’re positioning yourself at the heart of the next chapter of enterprise technology.
I’m rooting for you. Always!
