Introduction: Navigating the early-stage funding landscape for AI and SaaS startups
Early-stage fundraising has never been more mission-critical for AI and SaaS founders. Amid tightening markets—pre-seed and seed in Europe were on track for an 18% year-over-year decline as of Q1 2025, a signal of a broader capital bottleneck for early teams—founders need sharper strategies and the right partners to secure their first checks and build momentum ( Development Corporate ). That’s where partnering with a purpose-built pre-seed investor makes all the difference.
Identifying the right pre-seed venture capital partner isn’t just about capital; it’s about surrounding your startup with conviction, alignment, and operational support from day one. For AI and SaaS startups, the “first check” often dictates the pace of product iteration, customer validation, and follow-on fundraising. Selectivity here directly influences your trajectory.
Technical founders and first-time entrepreneurs face unique hurdles: translating technical advantage into a compelling go-to-market story, building early traction while shipping an MVP, and running a tight process with investors. This guide breaks down how to find pre-seed investors writing first checks, how to approach them, and why founder-first firms like Redbud VC are uniquely positioned to help you accelerate.
Understanding the Pre-Seed Venture Funding Landscape
What are pre-seed venture funds and why they’re vital for AI and SaaS startups
Pre-seed venture funds provide the earliest institutional capital to founders—often before significant revenue or traction. For AI and SaaS startups, pre-seed is the inflection point: it funds initial product development, proof-of-concept demonstrations, early pilots, and the first go-to-market experiments. This funding helps startups crystallize problem-solution fit, validate ICP pain, and reach the signals that unlock seed rounds. Independent investor lists and market maps show a healthy universe of specialized pre-seed investors actively engaging with founders at this stage ( openvc.app ; Beta Boom ; Rho ; Evalyze.ai ).
Key characteristics of top pre-seed VCs in the US
Focus on first checks: The best pre-seed funds are comfortable underwriting risk at day zero and can lead or co-lead your round with conviction. Market maps and investor directories help you filter for “pre-seed” and “first check” specialists ( vc-mapping.gilion.com ).
Sector depth in AI and SaaS: Many top pre-seed investors concentrate on developer tools, AI infrastructure, applied AI, or vertical SaaS, pairing capital with deep networks and insight ( Metal ).
Operator mindset: Pre-seed partners who can help you hire product and GTM leaders, orchestrate design partners, and tune your growth engine accelerate learning cycles. You’ll also find formal operator/scout programs that route experienced operators to early teams ( SuperScout ).
Founder-first support: The earliest capital matters most when it comes with hands-on help—from intros to early customers to real-time feedback on pricing, packaging, and positioning.
Trends in early-stage investment: AI, SaaS, and first-time founders
AI remains a dominant theme, especially applied AI and AI tooling. Investors are concentrating on practical AI use cases with measurable ROI and defensibility through data, distribution, or workflow lock-in. Independent lists highlight the depth of active AI-focused investors and scouts founders can approach directly ( openvc.app AI list ).
B2B SaaS remains resilient at pre-seed, with investors favoring vertical SaaS and workflow-specific solutions where budgets are identifiable and adoption can be measured with product analytics from day one ( Metal ).
Many pre-seed investors eagerly back first-time founders, particularly technical builders with clear customer insights and early validation signals ( Rho ; Beta Boom ).

If you’re a technical founder or a first-time entrepreneur, Redbud VC is designed to be a catalytic pre-seed ally—not just an investor.

How Technical and Midwest Founders Can Secure Funding
Strategies for technical founders to raise as early-stage entrepreneurs
Clarify the problem with customer evidence: Pair your technical insight with proof the pain is urgent and monetizable—customer interviews, pilot LOIs, design partner feedback, and quantified workflow impact.
Showcase the solution with demos and data: Ship a demo or narrow-scope MVP and track usage metrics that signal habit formation or ROI. Anchor your pitch in real workflows and measurable outcomes.
Prove capital efficiency: Present a 12–18 month plan showing milestones you’ll unlock with pre-seed—pilot conversions, model performance thresholds, or first $100k ARR.
Assemble complementary skills: Technical excellence plus early GTM muscle win pre-seed. If you’re solo, map how you’ll augment with fractional PM, design, or growth.
Run a tight fundraising process: Parallelize outreach, cluster investor calls, and send crisp updates. This drives momentum and creates natural social proof ( Rho ; Beta Boom ).
Redbud VC partners with technical founders to refine the problem thesis, facilitate design partner conversations, shape GTM experiments, and build the early talent bench so you can raise efficiently.
VCs that invest in technical founders from the Midwest and regional support programs
Founders building outside coastal hubs can turn geography into an edge—access to industry customers, cost advantages, and talent loyalty. To navigate regional capital:
Tap regional investor directories: Curated lists make it easy to identify Midwest-focused angels and early funds and accelerate warm intros ( openvc.app: Chicago list ).
Use Midwest resources and databases: Explore directories that aggregate accelerators, funds, and grants by state and sector ( MidFund ).
Build local momentum, then go national: Pair regional wins—pilot customers, channel partners—with a strategic national investor outreach.
Redbud VC actively champions founders across the US, with a particular empathy for technical teams in the Midwest. We help you leverage regional customer access while opening national investor and operator networks.
VCs offering operator programs to hire product and GTM leaders
Some investors provide structured access to operators—fractional product leaders, GTM builders, and advisors—to compress your time-to-learning. Where to start:
Operator/scout ecosystems: Platforms catalog operator and scout programs that plug founders into specialized talent early ( SuperScout ).
Founder communities and matchmakers: Many curated lists and communities connect founders with advisors and fractional leaders quickly.
Redbud VC collaborates with a network of product and GTM operators to help you hire or contract key leaders at the right stage—so your PMF and repeatability loops spin faster.
Approaching VCs and Building Investor Relationships
What US pre-seed investors take meetings with first-time founders?
Plenty do—especially if you bring tight problem insight and early validation. Use filters on open directories to identify pre-seed investors explicitly open to first-time founders; many publish public theses and accept direct pitches ( openvc.app ; Evalyze.ai ). When you tailor your outreach and show traction signals, you’ll unlock meaningful conversations—even without a warm intro.
Best practices for founders’ meetings, pitch preparation, and relationship-building
Build a narrative around urgency and inevitability: Why now, why this wedge, and why your team?
Keep the deck crisp: Problem, solution, ICP, market, early proof, traction metrics, GTM plan, team, and clear use of funds.
Personalize outreach: Reference investor theses and portfolio patterns; tie your pitch to their focus ( Rho ).
Create momentum with updates: Share progress weekly or biweekly—pilot wins, usage curves, and learnings. Data builds conviction.
Redbud VC is responsive to thoughtful cold outreach and values founders who share signal-rich updates. We often engage before formal fundraising to help shape milestones that de-risk your seed.
Tips to raise and close your seed round effectively
Start pre-seed with the seed in mind: Define the 3–5 crisp milestones that will unlock your seed—e.g., design partner conversions, repeatable outbound engine, model performance KPIs.
Instrument early: Track activation, retention, payback, and unit economics. Even directional metrics signal discipline and learning velocity.
Rehearse diligence: Prepare materials in advance—customer references, product demo scripts, data room (metrics, hiring plan, docs).
Orchestrate your close: Time-bound the process, line up anchors and strategic angels, and confirm availability before you kick off ( Beta Boom ; EverythingStartups ).
Redbud VC helps founders plan milestone-driven fundraising and run a structured, confident process to close strong.
