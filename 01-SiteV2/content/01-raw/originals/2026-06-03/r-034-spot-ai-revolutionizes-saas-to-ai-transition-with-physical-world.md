Spot AI Revolutionizes SaaS-to-AI Transition with Physical World Agents
This title was summarized by AI from the post below.
Rory O'Driscoll
2mo
Report this post
SaaS-to-AI transitions are rare. Spot AI is pulling it off by building agents for the physical world the way Intercom did for customer support agents.
Rish Gupta
2mo
Edited
$2 trillion in software market cap wiped out in 30 days. SaaS is being eaten alive by AI agents - and our investor just told our entire company we're one of the few positioned to win what comes next.
Public SaaS growth rates have hit record lows. Median growth fell to ~12% by end of 2025. In early 2026, $2 trillion in software market cap disappeared in 30 days as AI agents began replacing entire product categories.
Jeremy Kaufmann from Scale Venture Partners broke it down for our team: SaaS ate all of software by 2022-2023. No new markets left. Growth stalled. But then AI changed the game - not by improving software, but by eating human work itself.
The case study everyone's watching is Intercom. They pivoted to AI agents, re-accelerated from ~10% to 25% YoY growth, and their Fin agent alone is approaching $100M in ARR. Jeremy says only a handful of SaaS companies in history pulled off this transition.
He believes Spot AI is one of them.
The numbers:
→ Agent ARR grew 1,000% in a single year
→ Overall business growth accelerated from 58% to 70% YoY
→ Burn multiple improved by 60% (getting efficient fast)
→ 1,000+ customers across manufacturing, retail, healthcare, education
→ More new video indexed daily than YouTube
But here's the real lesson: almost every AI agent today is digital - automating text, tickets, and code. We're building AI agents for the physical world. Cameras that see, reason, and act on factory floors, in warehouses, and at storefronts.
Hardware-enabled SaaS is the next frontier of vertical AI. It's harder to build. It's also harder to replicate. And that's exactly why it works.
Huge thanks to Jeremy Kaufman and Scale Venture Partners for the partnership, the push, and the conviction.
When your investor compares your trajectory to one of the only SaaS-to-AI success stories in history - you don't slow down.

It looks like we’re quietly recreating the “growth at all costs” era. But this time around, it’s disguised as AI.
Coming out of the 2020s, the SaaS industry went through a much-needed reckoning. We all started paying closer attention to the Rule of 40, durable growth, and scalable business models.
But with the explosion of AI startups and complex usage-based pricing, it feels like we’re sprinting right back into the fire.
Here’s the fundamental shift: traditional SaaS enjoyed 80% to 90% gross profit margins because software scaled beautifully. You wrote the code once, deployed it, and sold it a million times.
AI is different.
Every single time a user interacts with a product, there’s compute attached. For the first time in a while, software companies are dealing with a heavy Cost of Goods Sold. As a result, AI companies are operating at 40% profit margins (or worse) as they race to add revenue and market share, burning cash on compute costs to do it.
This creates a visibility problem. The issue isn’t just the shrinking margin itself, it’s the disconnect in the data.
Today, costs live in one system, and billing lives in another. To be honest, no one’s bridged this gap so far. But as long as that disconnect exists, figuring out your true margins, profitable customers, and sustainable pricing models is going to be a challenge.
RevOps teams are under massive pressure to iterate on usage-based and hybrid pricing models to keep up with AI demand. But when billing is disconnected from actual cost data, companies risk scaling a loss leader without realizing it.
This gap inevitably hits finance and the boardroom. CFOs need investor-grade financial data to guide their companies. Without a single source of truth, board meetings stop being strategic and devolve into debates over data accuracy.
So where do you stand?
Do you actually know your true margins at the customer level? Can you tie usage directly to cost and revenue in real time? Or are you scaling faster than your visibility?
What are you doing right now to close that gap?

If you are still building traditional SaaS in 2026, you are already obsolete.
Would you rather pay for software, or pay for a guaranteed result? 🚀

Seat-based pricing is dying.
For fifty years, enterprise software ran one playbook:
- Build the "everything for everyone" suite.
- Buy the competition.
- Charge by the seat. (I'm looking at you, Jira.)
It worked brilliantly… until it didn't. IDC says by 2028, 70% of vendors will be forced to reprice around consumption and outcomes. ( https://lnkd.in/ghnpwJTF )
That's not a pricing tweak.
That's a business-model extinction event.
And the solution isn't just building a bigger suite. It's orchestration: an AI engine that composes best-of-breed APIs and tools on the fly to provide exactly what the customer needs, when they need it, and no more.
- No seat tax.
- No shelfware.
- No three-year contract for features you'll use once.
Enterprises (Goliaths, if you will) are going to struggle with this. Procurement cycles, integration debt, excessively complex 'omni' data models, and fourteen layers of approval make them slow to compose, slow to swap, and slow to ship.
But small teams don't have those constraints.
Solo-founder share of new startups has climbed from 24% in 2019 to 36% today ( https://lnkd.in/ggmupcpV ). Dario Amodei puts 70-80% odds on one-person companies delivering 10,000-person-equivalent output this year ( https://lnkd.in/gnF2Qe2Q ).
The new moat isn't headcount, it's composability: stitching the right tool into your workflow at the right time...and well before the enterprise procurement team has had their morning danish and kickoff call.
The age of Goliath is over.
The age of the nimble orchestrator has begun.
What's one 'everything' tool you'd rip out tomorrow if you could?
