# Inference cost is going to be the next AI bottleneck. - LinkedIn

## clean_text

Inference cost is going to be the next AI bottleneck.
In 2024, OpenAI reportedly burned $2 billion on inference, the cost of every AI interaction - which scales exponentially with adoption.
Yet companies budget AI like one-time software purchases instead of the metered utility it actually is.
And the data agrees, Gartner warns of 500-1,000% cost overruns because organizations don't map usage patterns before deployment.
Inference increases happen when every workflow triggers real-time model calls instead of strategic batching.
Which calls for smarter deployment patterns.
For example, Customer support needs immediate responses during peak hours. But internal reporting can batch overnight during cheaper compute windows.
The key is mapping which contexts justify real-time costs versus scheduled processing.
Enterprise thinking needs to shift from "how capable" to "how often" and "for which contexts."
Strategic deployment frequency beats raw capability every time.
Always happy to get others' perspectives :) what's your organization optimizing for - AI capability or deployment frequency?
