# Forward Deployed Engineers in High Demand for AI Deployments

## clean_text

The most contested hire in tech right now is not a researcher.
The most contested hire in tech right now is not a researcher. It's the Forward Deployed Engineer.
OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, and Palantir are spending billions to put these engineers inside their customers' buildings. Postings for the role are up roughly 10x in eighteen months.
Here's why.
MIT looked at 300 enterprise AI projects last year. 95% moved the P&L by almost nothing. The models worked. The deployments didn't.
A model that can't talk to a legacy database, can't pass the customer's security review, and can't be run by the team that inherits it is a demo, not a system.
The FDE is the person who closes that gap. Part engineer, part operator. The one who sits in the customer's mess until the thing runs in production.
That profile is rare on purpose. Most strong engineers want to build product, not deploy it. Most client facing people can't write the code. The FDE needs both, and a job posting can't screen for it.
In manufacturing for example.
Same gap. Predictive maintenance, defect detection, scheduling. Pilots that stall at the integration wall. Except the plant chasing that engineer is bidding against lab packages that clear half a million, and most don't even know the profile they're looking for.
This is the work we've done at WinMax for twenty years. Deploying hybrid engineers. The ones who stand between the floor and the model and make both move.
AI in manufacturing won't be won by whoever buys the best software. It'll be won by whoever can staff the people who make it land.
