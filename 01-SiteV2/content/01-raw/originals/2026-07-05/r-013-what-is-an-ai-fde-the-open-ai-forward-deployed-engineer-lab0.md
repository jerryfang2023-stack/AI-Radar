FAQ AI FDE, common questions
What is an AI FDE?
An AI FDE (AI forward-deployed engineer) is software that does the work a human forward-deployed engineer does, turning bought enterprise software into a working deployment, but delivered as a product instead of a person. It runs discovery, configuration, integration, and testing across the systems a company already owns, taking rollouts live in weeks instead of months.
What does an AI FDE do?
An AI FDE runs the enterprise software implementation lifecycle end to end: it gathers requirements through AI interviews and process mapping, configures the platform, builds and wires integrations, and validates every change against test cases before go-live. The same standardized agent workflow runs each rollout, so it gets faster the more it runs.
How is an AI FDE different from a human forward-deployed engineer?
A human FDE rebuilds the same discovery, configuration, and integration work by hand for every account, so cost scales with headcount. An AI FDE encodes that work as a reusable product: it standardizes the workflow, runs across many rollouts, and compresses 3–12 month implementations into weeks without adding people per deal.
Is an AI FDE the same as Palantir's AI FDE?
No. Palantir's AI FDE operates inside Palantir Foundry. Lab0's AI FDE is open and multi-platform, it runs implementation across the software customers already bought, including ServiceNow, SAP, Salesforce, Workday, and custom APIs, with no proprietary platform required.
What platforms does an AI FDE support?
Lab0's AI FDE is platform-agnostic. It works against the consoles an implementation team already uses, enterprise suites like ServiceNow, SAP, Salesforce, and Workday, as well as smaller B2B SaaS, internal tools, and bespoke APIs. If a system has a console or an API, the AI FDE can configure and integrate it.
How does an AI FDE keep changes safe?
Every change runs as a controlled dry-run preview before it writes to a live customer instance. Lab0's AI FDE uses a deterministic-versus-AI split for where human judgment is needed, and approval and rollback stay with the customer's team.
