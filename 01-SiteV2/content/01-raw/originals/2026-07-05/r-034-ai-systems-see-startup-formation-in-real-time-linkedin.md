# AI Systems See Startup Formation in Real Time - LinkedIn

## clean_text

Investors see snapshots. AI systems see sequences. And the sequence may be where the signal lives.
For decades, early-stage venture capital has been forced to underwrite raw human potential using thin proxies: resumes, elite schools, former employers, pitch decks, and a handful of meetings.
The problem is that startups aren't built in snapshots. They're built through thousands of small decisions made in isolation over months and years.
Until recently, that messy, iterative process was largely invisible to the outside world.
Today, founders spend hours working with AI as a core thinking partner, exploring markets, testing assumptions, refining strategy, and learning in real time.
In the process, they're creating something that has never really existed before: a longitudinal record of how they think.
That raises an interesting possibility: AI companies may have visibility into startup formation that no investor has ever had.
I wrote a longer piece exploring what this structural shift could mean for early-stage investing and why the same systems that improve prediction may also become worse at recognizing the next category-defining outlier.
