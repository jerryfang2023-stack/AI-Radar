# Tailored Automation With Vertical AI Agents | SS&C Blue Prism

What Is the Future of Work With Vertical AI Agents?
As artificial intelligence (AI), generative AI and automation software continue to adapt and improve at the enterprise level, specialized use cases are becoming more prevalent. Leaders want their business AI to solve specific problems both intelligently and securely.
Vertical AI is an AI application that is tailor-made for a specific industry and use case. It’s customized to meet specific challenges within a designated vertical sector. Meanwhile, horizontal AI platforms are general-purpose AI designed for a range of uses across various fields, like OpenAI’s enterprise solutions.
How Do Vertical AI Agents Work?
Vertical AI agents are designed with industry-specific domain expertise. This allows them to perform a nuanced task within a particular use case. For example, understanding regulatory requirements to prevent financial crime in a financial services organization.
Key characteristics of vertical AI:
Fine-tun ed : Vertical AI solutions are originally designed for a specific application, not adapted to fit a workflow from a larger set of capabilities. This level of specificity enables precise, relevant solutions.
Optimized : By using highly specialized first and third-party data, vertical AI (sometimes called specialized ai agents ) can improve process accuracy and performance far more than general AI tools with a mix of unfocused or general data.
Fully integrated: Vertical AI can integrate with existing systems and tools to make workflows run more efficiently, rather than disrupt them.
Domain expertise: This AI is built with an in-depth understanding of niche processes and business compliance standards, ensuring there’s always an expert in the workflow.
What Are the Benefits of Vertical AI Agents?
The benefits of specialized AI:
Tailor to your specific industry: AI systems designed for one purpose can make a bigger impact through detailed, knowledgeable decisions and actions. In using the right data sets and designing applications that can fit into your existing workflows, business users can also quickly adopt and take advantage of AI’s capabilities.
Keep your competitive edge : AI enables many competitive advantages, including faster, more intelligent workflows.
Enhance productivity : AI agents require fewer resources and less time to handle tasks, meaning they can do more in a shorter amount of time to make activities more productive.
Create smarter business operations : Vertical AI makes better use of your business functions by optimizing every step in a specific process.
Improve customer experience : These agents are honed into one area of work, making them capable of adapting to circumstances in real-time, such as analyzing customer buying behavior to provide more personalized marketing.
Get a better return on investment (ROI) : This specialized AI is made for a certain type of work, meaning it’s easier to predict how it can make improvements and earn a quick ROI.
Discover niche opportunities : Markets that couldn’t always benefit from AI can now use vertical AI capabilities to create tailored solutions with a big impact.
Scale across the business: AI agents can scale on demand without a proportional increase in headcount, which can especially help smaller organizations achieve significant growth at a lower cost.
What Are the Challenges With Vertical AI Agents?
Even with the specificity of vertical AI, companies still need to plan their AI integration to make it successful. Account for cost, resources, people, infrastructure and systems when searching for an AI solution that suits your business needs.
Responsible AI
Whether your AI models are trained on a large language model (LLM) or a small language model (SLM) , it’s important to implement strategic, responsible AI practices. This will protect your organization by keeping the AI algorithms accurate, unbiased and compliant.
Tools like SS&C | Blue Prism® Enterprise AI can act as a guardrail to your AI by combining orchestration, AI, automation and other tools with the highest levels of data security.
Technical complexity
Developing an accurate and reliable AI system can be a technical challenge without the right partner, tools and strategy to guide the design. Building effective vertical AI agents also requires deep domain knowledge to ensure accuracy and completeness when training the model.
Adoption resistance
Some teams may be hesitant to embrace AI technologies for fear that they’ll take over human jobs. To overcome these worries, enact change management initiatives where you educate employees on how the AI tools can assist their work. Encourage them to explore innovative new solutions and ways of working with vertical AI agents.
What Industries Are Vertical AI Agents Being Used In?
Since a vertical AI worker is designed with a specific industry use case in mind, it can really fit in wherever you build it, so long as you customize it to that workflow. Below, we’ll look at some industry examples.
What are the use cases of vertical AI agents?
Healthcare AI Agents : AI agents can integrate with electronic health records (EHRs) to speed up processes like managing patient data or scheduling appointments. They can even support diagnoses by analyzing patient medical history and other treatment data.
Customer service AI Agents: Vertical AI agents can manage routine inquiries and transactions with a personal touch using customer history. Examples include handling returns, recommending products, tracking deliveries and answering common queries.
Financial Services AI Agents : AI is well equipped to handle large datasets quickly and accurately, making it perfect for complex compliance and risk management tasks. Using advanced AI algorithms, vertical AI agents can monitor transactions and flag any anomalies that might indicate fraudulent activity.
Manufacturing AI Agents : Manual quality assurance processes can be time-consuming and prone to human error, but with a specifically built AI solution, the AI agent can improve development cycles and reduce errors.
How do I identify the right vertical processes?
If you’re looking to implement a vertical AI solution, start with:
Repetitive administrative tasks: Work like data entry, approvals, customer inquiries or meeting scheduling are prime automation candidates.
Real challenges : Look at your current processes and determine where they could most improve so your automation efforts focus on solving real pain points in the business.
A domain you know: Find an area in the business you know well so you can easily tailor a specific solution.
What Is the Future of Work With Vertical AI Agents?
The market predicts a future of AI where AI workers complete activities on their own without requiring human involvement. Agentic process automation or “ agentic AI ” is a nascent technology where AI agents autonomously act and make decisions for the business.
The benefits of autonomous automation are obvious – fewer resources, quicker processes, less strain on employees and smarter operations. But the foundation for such intricate technology requires robust AI governance and a framework to guide the planning and infrastructure setup.
What is the future of vertical AI agents?
As we see general-purpose AI reduce, the rise of vertical AI will take its place. Many experts are lauding the potential of vertical AI agents as the next evolution of software-as-a-service (SaaS), improving its scale and value, and transforming entire industries and traditional job roles. SaaS transformed software by making tools more accessible, scalable and easier to manage. Vertical AI has evolved those capabilities to automate entire workflows.
The impact of AI in business will be far from small. Ensure your organization is ready for the evolving AI workplace with SS&C Blue Prism’s AI-powered platform as your guiding beacon.
