Two weeks ago I posted about helping companies move toward AI-First R&D.
Since then I've sat with dozens of teams across startups, scale-ups, and enterprises. Here's what actually hurts:
Tooling paralysis is real:
Every week there's a new model, a new IDE plugin, a new "game changer". The teams that freeze trying to pick the "right" stack are the ones falling behind. The ones moving fastest picked something reasonable and committed.
Observability took a hit nobody expected:
Human PRs used to tell a story. You could read a developer's thinking in the diff. AI-assisted PRs are often polished blobs that tell you nothing about how someone actually thinks or works. Code review culture needs to evolve alongside the tooling and in-development observability for AI usage.
Security and IP are the quiet blocker:
Even teams in completely unregulated industries are nervous. What does the model retain? Who owns the output? These questions don't have clean answers yet, and they're killing adoption from the inside or make it an excruciatingly long process.
None of this means slow down. It means the transformation needs a real plan, not just a "we use Claude now" announcement.
If you're navigating any of this, hit me up
