# Designing a connected system for venture firm operations - LinkedIn

## clean_text

A funny thing about “platform” in venture: we talk about founders as systems thinkers, and then we design our own firms like silos.
A funny thing about “platform” in venture: we talk about founders as systems thinkers, and then we design our own firms like silos.
That VP of RevOps who helped you diligence a deal last quarter is going to become a design partner for one of your portcos. Then hire three reps out of your portfolio two years from now. If you do not have shared context across those moments, you are giving up compounding value.
Over the last few years, almost every firm I know has added platform headcount. At most of them, the work still runs in separate lanes: talent over here, BD over there, advisory and diligence somewhere else. All of them reaching into the same network of 200 operators and buyers with no shared context and data tracked across four separate spreadsheets.
They built platform functions but skipped the system design.
At Scale Venture Partners, we're hiring our first dedicated talent lead with a different starting point: network design. Who do we know? What do they care about? Where are they truly world-class? For any given person, the right mode might be: hire, diligence, feedback loop, customer intro or something we haven't named yet. The job is to know which one it is and when.
The work also requires being honest with the people in your orbit. You have to be explicit about why engaging is worth their time and equally explicit about what you're asking for in return. That is how you earn the right to ask again.
My hunch is that the firms that get the most from platform will stop organizing around functions and start asking a different question. If our network were a product, how would we design it? One connected system with context on every relationship, and a simple test before any ask – do we know exactly why this should be valuable for them and for us?
