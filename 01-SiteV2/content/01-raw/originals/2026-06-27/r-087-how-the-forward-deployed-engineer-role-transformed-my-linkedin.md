# How the Forward Deployed Engineer role transformed my ... - LinkedIn

## clean_text

I came across the concept of “Forward Deployed Engineer” for the first time during a conversation with one of our Co-Founders.
I came across the concept of “Forward Deployed Engineer” for the first time during a conversation with one of our Co-Founders. That conversation shifted how I think about my own role. Until then, I only knew myself as an Associate Solution Architect - someone who sits between product, engineering, and customers in an Applied AI company.
But whenever I tried explaining my work to friends, I often got:
“Wait… so you gather requirements?”
“Do you just fill spreadsheets?”
“Isn’t that just documentation?”
I used to laugh it off. But deep down I knew the work was so much more. I just didn’t have the language for it. Then I started learning about the Forward Deployed Engineer (FDE) role. And everything clicked.
FDEs are engineers who embed with customers to make AI actually work in their real environment. They translate messy workflows into functioning AI systems, iterate with users, solve problems in the wild, and ensure the product creates real business value.
And I realized… that’s exactly what we do as Solution Architects in Phenom.
We:
✨ dig into customer workflows
✨ map their pain points
✨ design AI-enabled solutions
✨ work hands-on with product and engineering
✨ drive adoption and user success
✨ and make sure the customer actually sees value
Most importantly:
We make customers stay.
Not because of contracts.
Not because of demos.
But because the solution works for them in the real world reliably, meaningfully, and every day.
Understanding the FDE role didn’t change my job. It changed how I see my job.
It gave structure to the hybrid, sometimes messy, always impactful nature of what I do.
And honestly, it made me appreciate this role even more.
We’re not “filling spreadsheets.”
We’re the bridge between AI technology and the people who depend on it.
We’re the reason the platform feels intuitive, solves real problems, and becomes part of the customer’s daily workflow.
And whether you call it Solution Architect, FDE, Applied AI Engineer, or anything else it’s the impact that matters.
