Introduction to Building AI Agents for Microsoft Marketplace
Feedback
Summarize this article for me
Startups that partner with Microsoft for Startups gain a strategic advantage by building on the Microsoft stack from day one. This alignment positions them for success in the B2B enterprise market, particularly in highly regulated industries such as financial services and healthcare. Microsoft is a recognized leader in enterprise-grade security, compliance, and identity management—capabilities that are critical for startups aiming to meet the rigorous demands of enterprise customers.
Beyond technical alignment, Microsoft for Startups offers a powerful go-to-market (GTM) pathway. Startups can tap into Microsoft's global customer base, including the 320 million daily active users of Microsoft Teams. By building agentic applications that integrate with Teams and Microsoft 365 using the Microsoft 365 Agents Toolkit, startups can deliver intelligent, conversational experiences directly into enterprise workflows.
This document outlines several approaches for startups to build, deploy, and monetize agentic applications through the Microsoft Marketplace. It walks through the steps to leverage Microsoft for Startups and the Microsoft Marketplace ecosystem to expand GTM reach, accelerate adoption, and drive revenue growth.
Microsoft for Startups Marketplace Publishing Support
The Microsoft for Startups program offers a wide range of go-to-market benefits, including accelerated publishing support for the Microsoft Commercial Marketplace. Because the process can be complex—requiring backend integration, compliance, and SKU logic—our team provides hands-on guidance every step of the way. We also connect startups to accelerator programs such as ISV Success and marketplace enablers like WeTransact. ISV Success delivers technical consultations and publishing support to help startups build and list transactable offers, while WeTransact provides a full-stack platform that automates publishing, billing, and co-sell orchestration—often getting startups live in under a week. Together, this support streamlines the publishing journey, reduces friction, and allows startups to stay focused on their product while unlocking new sales channels through the Commercial Marketplace.
Microsoft Marketplace Overview
Leveraging Microsoft Marketplace empowers AI agent publishers to reach enterprise customers at scale by making their solutions easily discoverable, transactable, and deployable within Microsoft 365 environments. By architecting agents as multi-tenant SaaS solutions hosted on Azure, publishers retain full control of their intellectual property while benefiting from Microsoft’s secure, compliant infrastructure and seamless integration with Microsoft 365 productivity tools like Teams and Copilot. The marketplace streamlines customer acquisition through direct subscription models, enabling rapid monetization and adoption across organizations that already trust Microsoft’s ecosystem.
Startups that sell their agent as a SaaS offer in the Microsoft Marketplace will benefit from easier procurement, stronger customer value, improved profitability, and expanded growth opportunities. Startups can reduce buyer friction by leveraging customers’ existing Microsoft procurement relationships, eliminating lengthy purchasing processes. Marketplace sales also directly benefit customers, since 100% of the price of a qualified offer counts toward their Azure consumption commitment (MACC). On the profitability side, startups save on billing and payment tools by using Microsoft’s commerce capabilities for a low 3% flat fee, with renewal fees reduced by 50%. Finally, publishing in the marketplace enables startups to scale through Cloud Solution Providers (CSPs), who can resell their solutions and open doors to new markets. Together, these advantages make the commercial marketplace a powerful channel for startups to drive adoption, revenue, and long-term growth.
The platform also provides built-in support for trials, tiered pricing, and co-selling opportunities with Microsoft, helping startups accelerate growth, drive revenue, and continuously improve their offerings through feedback and analytics.
This guide assumes you have joined the Microsoft AI Cloud Partner Program, enrolled in the commercial marketplace, and set up your tax and payment profile. If not, see the Marketplace Partner Playbook (Steps 1-4). In addition, enrolling in the Microsoft 365 and Copilot program will enable you to publish agents for Microsoft 365 and Copilot.
Recommended Marketplace Offer Types for AI Agents
The Microsoft Marketplace supports several offer types for publishing AI agent solutions, each suited to different deployment and customer scenarios.
SaaS offers are recommended for Teams or Copilot agents targeting broad enterprise adoption and are ideal for publisher-hosted solutions requiring custom UX or compute-intensive workloads, with flexible billing models and high control over the runtime environment. This control ensures that your model and code remain secure within your infrastructure, minimizing the risk of IP theft.
Solutions that must be deployed into the customer tenant, a combination of the Azure Application offer and the Container Offer. The Azure Application offer will act as a deployment wrapper for your solution. The Container Offer can be deployed into your Azure Application to provide granular metering.
Selecting the right offer type depends on the agent’s architecture, customer needs, desired billing strategy, and the level of control required to safeguard your intellectual property. Here are other offer types to consider, see flowchart for more details.
SaaS Offer Type
The SaaS (Software-as-a-Service) offer type is the preferred model for publishing enterprise-ready AI agents in the Microsoft commercial marketplace. With SaaS, the solution is hosted and managed by the publisher on Azure, allowing customers to subscribe and access the agent directly through the marketplace without the need for complex deployments. This approach supports multi-tenant architecture, streamlined billing, and easy onboarding, while enabling features like free trials, tiered pricing, and MACC (Microsoft Azure Consumption Commitment) eligibility. SaaS offers maximize scalability, security, and integration with Microsoft 365 environments, making them ideal for startups seeking to monetize and grow their AI agent solutions with enterprise customers. See Create a SaaS offer for more detail.
Pros of SAAS Offer Type
Global Market Access - Microsoft Marketplace connects partners with 4+ million monthly active visitors across 140+ geographies.
Azure Credits & Incentives - Partners receive Azure credits for development and testing, with Azure credits available once reaching certain marketplace sales.
Microsoft Field Seller Alignment - Access to Microsoft's 35,000 global field sellers who are incentivized to co-sell marketplace solutions.
Marketplace Rewards Tiers - Performance-based benefit tiers that unlock additional marketing, sales enablement, and technical resources as marketplace sales grow.
Analytics - Real-time performance analytics, customer feedback integration, and automated lifecycle management for continuous SaaS solution enhancement through shared technical infrastructure.”
Scalability: Multi-tenant architecture allows you to serve many customers from a single Azure-hosted backend, supporting growth without major infrastructure changes.
Streamlined Monetization: Integrated billing supports free trials, tiered pricing, metered billing, private offers, and paid plans, making it simple to manage subscriptions and revenue.
MACC Eligibility: Enterprise customers can use their Microsoft Azure Consumption Commitment budgets to purchase your solution, driving adoption in large organizations.
Security & Compliance: Azure provides robust identity management, data isolation, encryption, and compliance certifications, helping you meet enterprise requirements.
Cons of SAAS Offer Type
Publisher Responsibility: You must manage hosting, maintenance, and updates for all customers, which can require significant operational resources.
Certification Requirements: Passing Microsoft’s marketplace certification involves meeting strict security, compliance, and Responsible AI guidelines, which may require additional development effort.
Pricing Complexity: Setting competitive and profitable pricing can be challenging, especially with variable Azure OpenAI costs and evolving market standards.
Limited Customization for Individual Tenants: SaaS offers are designed for broad, multi-tenant use; customers needing highly customized or isolated deployments may require alternative offer types (e.g., Managed Applications).
Azure Application Offer - Container Offer Combination
The Azure Application and Container offer combination allows publishers to deliver AI agent solutions that are deployed and operated entirely within the customer’s own Azure subscription. This model is ideal for enterprise customers who require strict control over data residency, security, and compliance, as all resources and data remain within their environment. Azure Application provides a packaged deployment experience, including automated setup and lifecycle management, while enabling the publisher to offer ongoing support and updates. Although this approach offers maximum customization and isolation for individual tenants, it is less optimal for scalable Teams and Copilot scenarios, where multi-tenant SaaS architectures are preferred for broad reach and streamlined management. Learn more about publishing Azure Container-based agents.
Pros of Container-based deployment
Customer Control: All resources and data are deployed within the customer’s own Azure subscription, giving enterprises maximum control over data residency, security, and compliance.
Customizability: Solutions can be tailored to meet specific customer requirements, including unique configurations, integrations, or compliance needs.
Isolation: Each customer’s deployment is isolated, reducing risks of cross-tenant data leakage and supporting strict regulatory requirements.
Lifecycle Management: Managed Applications provide automated setup, updates, and lifecycle management, simplifying ongoing maintenance for both publisher and customer.
Enterprise Trust: This approach may be preferred by organizations with heightened security or governance policies, increasing trust and adoption among regulated industries.
Cons of Container-based deployment
Limited Scalability: Each customer requires a separate deployment, which can increase operational overhead and reduce efficiency compared to multi-tenant SaaS models.
Complex Support: Supporting multiple isolated environments can be more complex and resource-intensive for publishers, especially as the customer base grows.
Slower Onboarding: Customers must provision and manage their own Azure resources, which can slow down onboarding and increase friction compared to instant SaaS subscriptions.
Marketplace Commerce Limitations: Azure Container offers may not support all marketplace commerce features (such as MACC eligibility, private offers, or long-term billing) as seamlessly as SaaS offers.
Less Ideal for Copilot Agents: For Copilot scenarios targeting broad enterprise use, Azure Container offers are generally less optimal than SaaS offers, as they hinder integration and rapid scaling.

This document outlines several approaches for startups to build, deploy, and monetize agentic applications through the Microsoft Marketplace. It walks through the steps to leverage Microsoft for Startups and the Microsoft Marketplace ecosystem to expand GTM reach, accelerate adoption, and drive revenue growth. The Microsoft for Startups program offers a wide range of go-to-market benefits, including accelerated publishing support for the Microsoft Commercial Marketplace. Because the process can be complex—requiring backend integration, compliance, and SKU logic—our team provides hands-on guidance every step of the way. We also connect startups to accelerator programs such as ISV Success and marketplace enablers like WeTransact. ISV Success delivers technical consultations and publishing support to help startups build and list transactable offers, while WeTransact provides a full-stack platform that automates publishing, billing, and co-sell orchestration—often getting startups live in under a week. Together, this support streamlines the publishing journey, reduces friction, and allows startups to stay focused on their product while unlocking new sales channels through the Commercial Marketplace. Leveraging Microsoft Marketplace empowers AI agent publishers to reach enterprise customers at scale by making their solutions easily discoverable, transactable, and deployable within Microsoft 365 environments. By architecting agents as multi-tenant SaaS solutions hosted on Azure, publishers retain full control of their intellectual property while benefiting from Microsoft’s secure, compliant infrastructure and seamless integration with Microsoft 365 productivity tools like Teams and Copilot. The marketplace streamlines customer acquisition through direct subscription models, enabling rapid monetization and adoption across organizations that already trust Microsoft’s ecosystem. Startups that sell their agent as a SaaS offer in the Microsoft Marketplace will benefit from easier procurement, stronger customer value, improved profitability, and expanded growth opportunities. Startups can reduce buyer friction by leveraging customers’ existing Microsoft procurement relationships, eliminating lengthy purchasing processes. Marketplace sales also directly benefit customers, since 100% of the price of a qualified offer counts toward their Azure consumption commitment (MACC). On the profitability side, startups save on billing and payment tools by using Microsoft’s commerce capabilities for a low 3% flat fee, with renewal fees reduced by 50%. Finally, publishing in the marketplace enables startups to scale through Cloud Solution Providers (CSPs), who can resell their solutions and open doors to new markets. Together, these advantages make the commercial marketplace a powerful channel for startups to drive adoption, revenue, and long-term growth. SaaS offers are recommended for Teams or Copilot agents targeting broad enterprise adoption and are ideal for publisher-hosted solutions requiring custom UX or compute-intensive workloads, with flexible billing models and high control over the runtime environment. This control ensures that your model and code remain secure within your infrastructure, minimizing the risk of IP theft. The SaaS (Software-as-a-Service) offer type is the preferred model for publishing enterprise-ready AI agents in the Microsoft commercial marketplace. With SaaS, the solution is hosted and managed by the publisher on Azure, allowing customers to subscribe and access the agent directly through the marketplace without the need for complex deployments. This approach supports multi-tenant architecture, streamlined billing, and easy onboarding, while enabling features like free trials, tiered pricing, and MACC (Microsoft Azure Consumption Commitment) eligibility. SaaS offers maximize scalability, security, and integration with Microsoft 365 environments, making them ideal for startups seeking to monetize and grow their AI agent solutions with enterprise customers. See Create a SaaS offer for more detail. - Global Market Access - Microsoft Marketplace connects partners with 4+ million monthly active visitors across 140+ geographies. - Azure Credits & Incentives - Partners receive Azure credits for development and testing, with Azure credits available once reaching certain marketplace sales. - Microsoft Field Seller Alignment - Access to Microsoft's 35,000 global field sellers who are incentivized to co-sell marketplace solutions. - Streamlined Monetization: Integrated billing supports free trials, tiered pricing, metered billing, private offers, and paid plans, making it simple to manage subscriptions and revenue - MACC Eligibility: Enterprise customers can use their Microsoft Azure Consumption Commitment budgets to purchase your solution, driving adoption in large organizations. The Azure Application and Container offer combination allows publishers to deliver AI agent solutions that are deployed and operated entirely within the customer’s own Azure subscription. This model is ideal for enterprise customers who require strict control over data residency, security, and compliance, as all resources and data remain within their environment. Azure Application provides a packaged deployment experience, including automated setup and lifecycle management, while enabling the publisher to offer ongoing support and updates. Although this approach offers maximum customization and isolation for individual tenants, it is less optimal for scalable Teams and Copilot scenarios, where multi-tenant SaaS architectures are preferred for broad reach and streamlined management.
