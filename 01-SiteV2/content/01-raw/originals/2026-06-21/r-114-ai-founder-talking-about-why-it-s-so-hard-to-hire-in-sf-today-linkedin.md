The hiring difficulty in SF makes sense when you consider the misalignment between what engineers and founders are optimizing for.
As someone who’s thought about both sides: if I were evaluating AI companies, I’d prioritize learning opportunities and meaningful problem-solving over compensation. The financial upside follows naturally if you’re building something that matters.
That said, I think founders need to be realistic too. Talented engineers have options, and while you shouldn’t lead with compensation demands, it’s fair to expect competitive offers that reflect both market rates and the risk you’re taking on equity-heavy packages. “Prove yourself first” can come across as one-sided when founders are also asking engineers to prove their vision is worth the bet.
The key is mutual commitment: engineers willing to invest in the company’s success, and founders who recognize that means fair compensation + genuine growth opportunities. The best hires happen when both sides see it as a partnership, not a transaction.
Being too rigid on either side just means losing out to competitors who get this balance right.
