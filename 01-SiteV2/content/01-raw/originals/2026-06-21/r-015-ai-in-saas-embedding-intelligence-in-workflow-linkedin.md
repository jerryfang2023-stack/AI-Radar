AI Office Hours Notes #1
AI Office Hours Notes #1
Today I had an interesting session during my AI Weekend Office Hours with an early-stage startup founder.
Their product has been live for close to a year and already has customers actively using it. The team has built a solid SaaS platform that helps customers manage operational data and workflows. The question the founder brought to the table was simple: “Should we add an AI copilot inside our product?”
The reason behind the question was familiar. Investors, prospects, and even some customers had started asking, “Where is the AI in your product?” So the team had begun exploring options like adding a chatbot, summarizing dashboards with AI, or generating automated reports.
When we walked through how customers actually use the product, an interesting pattern emerged. Users spend a significant amount of time interpreting dashboards and operational data before deciding what action to take. The platform already captures the data well, but the decision-making step still sits entirely with the user.
The initial mistake was trying to think about AI as a feature to add, rather than as intelligence embedded in the workflow.
My recommendation was to start their AI journey with a use case that directly supports decision-making in the workflow. Instead of building a chatbot or another interface, the product could surface signals such as potential operational risks, supplier delays, or upcoming inventory shortages, and suggest possible actions. This would help users move from data to decisions much faster.
I also suggested that while there will certainly be many more AI opportunities they can add to their roadmap over time, starting with this use case will likely drive immediate value for their existing customers and accelerate adoption. It is a practical way for the team to begin their AI journey with something that naturally fits into how the product is already being used.
I’ll continue sharing a few interesting insights from these conversations as part of this AI Office Hours Notes series. Many founders are navigating similar questions as they try to understand where AI genuinely belongs in their product.
