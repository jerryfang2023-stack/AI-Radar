# The Limitations of Forward Deployed Engineers in AI Integration

## clean_text

The rise of the Forward Deployed Engineer (#FDE) is one of the clearest signals in the AI market right now - even spawning new billion dollar enterprises from OpenAI and Anthropic.
The rise of the Forward Deployed Engineer (#FDE) is one of the clearest signals in the AI market right now - even spawning new billion dollar enterprises from OpenAI and Anthropic. It reinforces that AI value does not appear just because capabilities exist. Models are powerful. Demos are impressive. Pilots are everywhere. But turning AI into operating value requires translation into real work, real systems, real constraints, and real workflows.
So the FDE makes sense. Or does it?
Move technical talent closer to the customer. Let engineers see the workflow, understand the context, build the integration, and close the gap between what AI can do and what the enterprise can actually use. Feels like a useful response?!
But I don’t think it is the whole answer, or potentially addressing the biggest source of drag. The biggest gap is not between the AI company and the customer. The bigger gap is between AI capability and human #integration.
And that is where the FDE model still feels incomplete because it still starts with the technical expert. The expert studies the work. The expert builds the solution. The expert deploys the workflow. The human is expected to adopt. That may be faster than traditional implementation and may be smarter than generic enablement. It might even produce better technical fit and some near term gains.
But it still misses the #PeopleSideOfAI!!
People do not adopt AI because someone engineered something elegant. True AI Integration happens when people see its relevance and feel capable of using it to succeed in their own, specific work. It’s when they find their own You(se) Case, beyond another shiny Use Case. The people closest to the work are not just users of AI-enabled solutions. They are often the best source of insight about where AI can help, where it will fail, where judgment matters, and where the workflow itself needs to be reimagined.
So rather than asking: How do we forward-deploy engineers into the business? Maybe the better question is: How do we equip people to become better explorers, testers, and integrators of AI in their own work?
Deploying FDEs is still approaching AI from the technical side. A “Forward Deployed Integration Engineer” #FDIE equips people with the mindset and structure to ask: Where is this still my work? Where could AI work with me? Where could AI work for me?
(see Prosci AI Integration Framework for more)
FDEs may help close the implementation gap, but value depends on AI Integration which requires something more. Realizing enterprise AI value requires activating the people closest to the work as front-line AI integrators, not just recipients of deployment.
