AI-native (6–7%): usually funded startups that treat AI as the operating layer. Humans sit at the edge.
They ask “Which AI agent should we build next?” not “Who should we hire?” They move fast because AI is woven into product and ops from day one.
AI-forward (20–30%): industry leaders (think JP Morgan, Unilever, Amazon) running hundreds of pilots. Many experiments fail, but the few that win move the needle. These companies build AI literacy, accept intelligent risk, and institutionalize learnings.
AI-adjusting (the majority): firms just starting pilots or limited to approved copilots. Adoption is scattered, some teams resist, and leadership struggles with change management. Here the barriers are human fear, misalignment, and poor rollout design.
The takeaway: your AI tactics must match your company type. Don’t copy a startup playbook if you’re AI-adjusting. Focus first on governance, owner-defined pilots, and communication. If you’re AI-forward, double down on fast experiments and scaling winners. If you’re AI-native, keep treating automation as product.
