# One raises $4M to build the trust layer for AI agents | One

## clean_text

One raises $4M to build the trust layer for AI agents
Bringing our total seed funding to $7M to make AI agents safe to deploy inside real enterprises.
Today we're announcing that One has raised $4 million in new seed funding to build a trust layer for AI agents, the system enterprises need to bring the agentic movement into production. The round brings our total seed funding to $7 million to date.
Our investors, Four Rivers , Ripple Ventures , Golden Ventures , Village Global , and the Hyatt family, share a conviction that the next decade of software will be built by agents, and that agents can't operate inside a business until they can be trusted to act on its behalf.
The problem: agents can reason, but they can't yet be trusted to act
AI models have gotten remarkably good at reasoning. What they still lack is a safe, governed way to do things: to connect to the tools a company already runs, take real actions across them, and do so under the permissions, audit, and control an enterprise can stand behind.
That gap is the single biggest thing standing between a promising demo and a deployed agent. Every serious company experimenting with agents runs into the same wall. Connecting to hundreds of systems is hard, and letting an autonomous agent touch those systems without guardrails is a non-starter.
What One does: the trust layer for AI agents
One is the trust layer between AI agents and the rest of a company's software.
We give agents a single, unified way to reach the tools businesses already depend on, and we wrap that access in the authentication, permissions, and auditability that make it safe to turn on in production.
500+ platforms connected through one interface: Gmail, Slack, Salesforce, Stripe, Shopify, HubSpot, Notion, and hundreds more.
80,000+ ready-to-use tools an agent can call, without teams having to build and maintain integrations themselves.
22,000 users already building on One, from solo builders to enterprise teams.
Built on 12 years of hard-won experience in the integration space, the exact problem that has to be solved right for agents to work.
The result: what used to take an engineering team months to wire up, an agent can now reach in minutes, safely.
Why now
The agentic movement is real, and it's moving fast. But adoption inside real companies is being throttled by trust, not by model quality. Enterprises won't hand an agent the keys to their systems until they can govern exactly what it can see and do.
We built One to remove that blocker, to be the layer that lets enterprises say yes to agents. Since our first seed in 2021, we've kept building through that thesis, and this new round lets us go all-in on it.
What the funding enables
With this raise, we're investing in:
Deepening the trust layer: richer permissioning, governance, and audit so enterprises can deploy agents with confidence.
Expanding coverage: more platforms, more actions, and deeper support for the systems large organizations actually run.
Enterprise readiness: the security, compliance, and reliability work that makes One a system a Fortune 500 can bet on.
Growing the team: hiring across engineering, product, and go-to-market to meet the demand we're already seeing.
Join us
If you believe agents are about to do real work inside real companies, and you want to build the infrastructure that makes that safe, we want to talk to you. We're hiring across the board.
And if you're building with agents today, you can start on One now at withone.ai .
The agentic movement needs a trust layer. We're building it.
The One Team
