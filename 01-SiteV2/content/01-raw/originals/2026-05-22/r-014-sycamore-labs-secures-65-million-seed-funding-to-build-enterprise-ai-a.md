## clean_text

Sycamore Labs Raises $65 Million in Seed Round
Palo Alto-based startup Sycamore Labs has announced a $65 million seed funding round, aiming to enhance its platform for building, deploying, and managing autonomous AI agents. These agents are designed with enterprise-grade security, human oversight, and the capability to grow organizational intelligence over time.
Investors and Leadership
The seed round was led by Coatue, with participation from Lightspeed Venture Partners, Abstract Ventures, Dell Technologies Capital, 8VC, Databricks Inc., OpenAI Group PBC, Intel, and Palo Alto Networks Inc. Sycamore Labs was founded by Sri Viswanath, who previously served as the Chief Technology Officer at Atlassian and a partner at Coatue. Viswanath brings over two decades of experience in building large-scale enterprise platforms. "The round came together through long-standing relationships," Viswanath mentioned, highlighting the depth of his network.
Sycamore's Vision for AI in Enterprises
Sycamore Labs is working on creating an 'agentic operating system' for enterprises. This system aims to allow businesses to manage AI agents at scale within a secure and governed environment. The platform addresses what Viswanath refers to as "operational gravity," where enterprises have experimented with AI in controlled settings but lack the infrastructure to deploy these agents safely on a larger scale.
"Most tools take existing workflows and layer agents on top," Viswanath explained. Sycamore's platform, however, "starts with the problem itself and then designs and builds the right solution from scratch."
Strategic Use of Funds
The $65 million raised will be utilized to further develop Sycamore's operating system, ensuring that enterprises can safely scale their use of AI agents. While specific plans for the funds were not disclosed, the focus appears to be on expanding the platform's capabilities and potentially growing the team to meet the demands of larger enterprise clients.
Competitive Landscape
Sycamore Labs enters a competitive market filled with startups aiming to integrate AI into enterprise workflows. Despite the crowded field, Sycamore's approach of building a comprehensive orchestration layer for AI agents distinguishes it from many competitors who focus on narrower solutions.
This significant seed funding round, bolstered by prominent Silicon Valley investors, positions Sycamore Labs as a notable player in the evolving landscape of enterprise AI solutions.
