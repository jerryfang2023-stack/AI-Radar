# One of the most interesting paradoxes in AI right now is this - LinkedIn

## clean_text

One of the most interesting paradoxes in AI right now is this:
One of the most interesting paradoxes in AI right now is this:
The cost of intelligence is collapsing but enterprise AI bills are exploding.
Inference costs have dropped dramatically over the past few years due to better models, hardware, and competition. At the same time, companies are spending more and more on AI every year.
Why is this happening?
Because we are moving from chatbots to agents and AI workflows:
🔵 A chatbot makes one model call.
🔵 An AI workflow may make ten or twenty.
🔵 Add retrieval, agents, monitoring, retries, background jobs, and suddenly one task becomes dozens of model calls.
The more useful AI becomes, the more inference you consume.
From a startup perspective, this is very important because many AI startups today are built on top of external model APIs – OpenAI or Anthropic, so their unit economics depend on inference costs they do not control. If pricing changes (and it will most likely change in the near future) or usage grows faster than expected, margins can disappear very quickly.
So the companies that will win are not the ones with the cheapest model calls. They are the ones that minimize tokens per task, use smaller models where possible, run inference locally when volume is high, and design workflows efficiently from day one.
In other words, inference economics is becoming a core competency. We are already seeing companies building tools for inference optimization and managing AI costs at scale (kudos to TheStage AI and Kirill Solodskih, Solodskikh). I think this will become one of the most important metrics in AI businesses over the next few years.
From a VC perspective, this infrastructure layer is becoming one of the key "picks and shovels" categories, alongside areas like regulation and compliance. Companies that help others run AI cheaper, faster, and more predictably may end up being some of the most important players in the ecosystem.
If you know companies working on inference optimization or AI infrastructure, feel free to tag them in the comments. This is a space I'm watching closely.
