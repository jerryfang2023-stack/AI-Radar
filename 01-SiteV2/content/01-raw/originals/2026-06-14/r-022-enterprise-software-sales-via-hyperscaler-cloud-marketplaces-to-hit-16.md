Enterprise Software Sales via Hyperscaler Cloud Marketplaces to Hit $163 bn by 2030: Omdia
07/10/2025
There will be a surge in enterprise software sales through hyperscaler cloud marketplaces, led by Amazon Web Services (AWS), Microsoft Azure, and Google Cloud.
According to a research from Omdia , marketplace sales are projected to climb from $30 billion in 2024 to $163 billion by 2030, driven by growing enterprise adoption of marketplace procurement and rapid expansion in agentic AI offerings.
Omdia forecasts a strong compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 29.1 percent between 2025 and 2030, as both global software giants and independent software vendors (ISVs) increasingly turn to hyperscaler marketplaces as a primary route to market.
Strategic Cloud Spending Drives Marketplace Growth
A major factor fueling this growth is the rise of multi-year cloud spending commitments by enterprise customers. Omdia estimates nearly $470 billion in total cloud commitments across AWS, Azure, and Google Cloud. A significant portion of this budget can now be spent on third-party software purchases through the hyperscaler marketplaces.
In the second quarter of 2025 alone, enterprises added nearly $30 billion in new cloud commitments, underscoring the momentum behind this procurement model. What started as an opportunistic way to utilize unused cloud credits is now becoming a strategic procurement approach, where enterprises negotiate cloud deals that include marketplace budgets aligned with their cloud transformation goals.
Channel Partners Thrive in Marketplace Ecosystem
Contrary to fears that cloud marketplaces would displace traditional partners, channel partners are adapting and thriving in this new model. Omdia predicts that by 2030, partners will facilitate nearly 60% of all marketplace transactions, leveraging private offers and distributor models introduced by hyperscalers.
Partners are increasingly helping customers manage cloud commitments, purchase across multiple marketplaces, and provide end-to-end lifecycle support. With the rise of agentic AI, many partners are also building their own AI-driven platforms and services, capturing a larger share of marketplace revenues.
Foundational Software Leads, AI and Security Gain Momentum
By 2025, infrastructure software ($10.5 billion), DevOps tools ($9.1 billion), and business applications ($9.1 billion) will account for nearly 63% of marketplace spending, according to Omdia. These foundational categories continue to underpin enterprise cloud environments and modernization initiatives.
However, the AI marketplace is emerging as a major growth engine. Driven by agentic AI applications and the rise of multi-agent protocols, AI-related transactions are forecasted to reach $24.4 billion by 2030, growing at a 37 percent CAGR.
Cybersecurity is another fast-growing segment, projected to hit $31 billion by 2030 with a 31% CAGR, as enterprises invest in integrated, AI-powered security solutions to safeguard hybrid and multi-cloud ecosystems.
Hyperscalers Compete to Lead the Agentic AI Channel
“Hyperscaler marketplaces continue to see rapid momentum as a route to market for vendors across the technology industry. A small but growing number of ISVs are now reaching — and exceeding — $1 billion in annual sales through AWS, Google Cloud, and Microsoft Azure marketplaces,” said Alastair Edwards, Chief Analyst at Omdia.
“Agentic AI will be one of the fastest-growing categories through marketplaces in the next five years. The hyperscalers are competing hard to win the race as a channel for agentic AI through their agent marketplaces, which are becoming a critical driver of cloud consumption,” Alastair Edwards said.
AWS Marketplace Initiatives
Private Offers and CPPO Program:
AWS introduced the Channel Partner Private Offers (CPPO) program, allowing independent software vendors (ISVs) to extend private pricing and custom contract terms through consulting partners. This enables partners to sell third-party software directly via the AWS Marketplace, helping enterprises align purchases with existing AWS cloud commitments.
AWS Marketplace for Containers and SaaS:
To support modern application development, AWS offers container-based solutions and SaaS subscriptions directly from its marketplace. Enterprises can deploy containerized apps on Amazon ECS, EKS, or Fargate with a single click.
Integration with AWS Cost Management Tools:
Enterprises can apply marketplace purchases toward their Enterprise Discount Programs (EDP), ensuring that third-party software spend contributes to meeting AWS spending commitments.
AI and ML Solutions Hub:
AWS has expanded its marketplace to include pre-trained AI and machine learning models, agentic AI tools, and data labeling services — enabling enterprises to accelerate AI adoption within existing AWS environments.
Microsoft Azure Marketplace Initiatives
Microsoft Commercial Marketplace (AppSource + Azure Marketplace):
Microsoft merged AppSource and Azure Marketplace into a unified commercial marketplace, enabling enterprise customers to discover and purchase SaaS, data, and AI solutions seamlessly integrated with Azure services.
Azure IP Co-sell Program:
Microsoft’s co-sell program incentivizes ISVs by offering joint sales motions with Microsoft sellers. This initiative helped generate billions in partner revenue by aligning Azure Marketplace solutions with Microsoft’s enterprise customer base.
Private Offers and Multi-Party Selling:
Microsoft introduced Private Offers and Multi-Party Private Offers (MPPO), allowing ISVs, distributors, and resellers to collaborate on customized deals, making enterprise procurement faster and more compliant.
Azure AI Gallery and Agentic AI Marketplace:
To capture AI-driven demand, Microsoft has added AI agents, copilots, and analytics platforms into its marketplace ecosystem. These solutions can be deployed directly within Azure’s cognitive and generative AI services.
Google Cloud Marketplace Initiatives
Integrated Billing and Spend Commitment Alignment:
Google Cloud allows enterprise customers to count marketplace purchases toward their committed cloud spend, simplifying financial governance and helping vendors gain traction among committed customers.
Google Cloud Partner Advantage and ISV Program:
Through Partner Advantage, Google enables ISVs and resellers to co-sell and co-market their offerings on the Google Cloud Marketplace, leveraging Google’s global sales and support network.
AI and Data Marketplace Expansion:
Google has built out its marketplace to feature Vertex AI models, agentic AI frameworks, and data analytics platforms, offering pre-built tools that integrate natively with BigQuery and Cloud AI.
Private Offers and Procurement APIs:
Google launched Procurement APIs that help enterprises automate approval workflows and integrate marketplace purchases into their internal procurement systems — a key feature for large organizations managing multi-cloud budgets.
Ecosystem-Wide Trends and Collaborative Initiatives
Multi-Cloud Marketplace Integrations:
Several ISVs, such as Snowflake, Palo Alto Networks, and Datadog, have built listings across AWS, Azure, and Google Cloud marketplaces, ensuring enterprises can purchase through their preferred hyperscaler while using existing spend commitments.
Joint GTM Programs with ISVs:
All three hyperscalers have launched joint go-to-market programs that include marketing funds, technical enablement, and sales alignment with ISV partners to accelerate marketplace growth.
AI and Security Alliances:
Vendors like CrowdStrike, ServiceNow, and Databricks are expanding their AI-powered solutions across multiple cloud marketplaces, taking advantage of co-selling incentives and integrated deployment support from hyperscalers.
