# AI-driven M&A shifts from growth to workflow control - LinkedIn

## clean_text

All the SpaceX news meant that a few momentous deals went almost unnoticed.
Salesforce’s $3.6B purchase of Fin is an illustration of how AI-driven M&A is no longer about experimentation or signalling. It is about buying capabilities that protect the core business and deepen customer workflow control.
AI in M&A is about really reshaping the deal thesis, not just a tool or some kind of technology add-on.
What used to be a question of “how do we buy growth?” had become “How do we buy data?” and is now becoming:
How do we buy workflow control?
How do we buy model advantage?
How do we buy integration speed?
In 2026, the most interesting acquisitions are not just about scale. They’re about capability acquisition — especially embedded AI workflows, talent, and proprietary data.
That changes a few things:
- Smaller, targeted deals can be more strategic than large platform buys. And they’re not going to feel small, if they pack an AI punch
- Integration risk matters even more, because AI value collapses fast if data, governance, or access controls are weak.
- Buyers need to think about post-close operating design from day one, not after signing.
The next wave of M&A won’t be driven only by synergies.
It will be driven by who can combine capital, data, and AI-enabled execution the fastest.
The best acquirers won’t just be buying companies. They’ll be buying future operating models.
