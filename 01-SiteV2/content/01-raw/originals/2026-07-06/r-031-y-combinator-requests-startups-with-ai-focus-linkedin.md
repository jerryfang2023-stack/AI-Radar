🏈 It's Super Bowl Sunday. But in Venture Capital, AI already won the championship.
While we're picking sides today, VC already picked its winner in 2025.
Artificial Intelligence. And it wasn't even close.
$211 billion flowed into AI last year — 50% of every venture dollar on the planet. Up 85% from 2024. The kind of blowout that makes a 43-8 Super Bowl look competitive.
Here's the tape:
→ Global VC hit $425B in 2025, up 30% YoY. Nearly all that growth? AI.
→ Five companies (OpenAI, Anthropic, xAI, Scale AI, Project Prometheus) raised $84B — 20% of ALL global VC.
→ 70% of U.S. venture dollars went to $100M+ rounds. This isn't a league anymore. It's the AI All-Stars running up the score.
Where's that $211B actually going? Think of it like a depth chart:
🔹 Foundation Models (~$80B) — The franchise QBs. OpenAI, Anthropic, xAI commanding 40% of all AI funding.
🔹 Chips & Silicon (~$12B) — The offensive line. Cerebras, Groq, and custom silicon making the plays possible.
🔹 Infrastructure & Compute (~$18B) — The stadium itself. Data centers, cloud, energy — what Jensen Huang calls "the largest infrastructure buildout in history."
🔹 Applications (~$19B revenue) — The skill players. Cursor, Perplexity, Harvey turning AI into products.
🔹 Robotics & Physical AI — The rookie class. Waabi ($750M), Zipline ($600M). 2026 could be their breakout year.
Meanwhile, everyone else is riding the bench. Crypto deal count dropped 60%. Biotech hit a 20-year funding low. SaaS without an AI story? Can't even get a meeting.
Big Tech's capex tells the same story — Alphabet, Amazon, Meta, and Microsoft are forecasting $650B combined for 2026. That's more than the 21 largest U.S. automakers, defense contractors, and railroads combined.
So is this a dynasty or a bubble?
The bull case: Enterprise AI revenue surged from $1.7B to $37B since 2023. Startups earn $2 for every $1 incumbents make. Real revenue is materializing.
The bear case: OpenAI may burn $140B by 2029 without a profit. The median AI startup burns $5 to earn $1. Even Sam Altman admits investors are "overexcited."
Here's my playbook for 2026:
🏈 Building AI? Capital is there, but 2026 rewards fundamentals — revenue, efficiency, real moats. Not AI veneer on old ideas.
🏈 NOT building AI? Find where AI intersects your domain. Defense tech, robotics, and healthcare AI are surging. An AI angle isn't optional anymore.
🏈 Investing? 12 firms collected 50%+ of all VC in H1 2025. Late-stage AI trades at 21-28x revenue vs 6x for SaaS. Upside is huge, but so is concentration risk.
The biggest question for 2026 isn't who wins today's game — it's whether AI produces a lasting dynasty or the most expensive halftime show in history.
Enjoy the game 🏈
