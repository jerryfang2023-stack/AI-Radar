AI governance startup pockets $4 million Seed round
Aigentsphere raises $4 million to launch governance system for the future AI workforce.
Simon Thomsen
Apr 29, 2026
2 min read
Share
Prefer Startup Daily
Aigentsphere CEO Quinton Anderson
Sydney AI agent management and governance platform, Aigentsphere, has raised a $4 million Seed round led by CSIRO-backed VC Main Sequence.
The funds will go to upping the engineering team and expanding operations in Australia and the United States.
Aigentsphere’s platform addresses potential AI agent sprawl with a unified control layer that enables organisations to register and onboard agents, monitor their performance in real time, track costs, enforce policies and automatically generate compliance reporting.
Chair Kelly Bayer Rosmarin said increasing demand for AI management and governance is being driven both by board-level mandates to innovate rapidly with AI, and regulatory pressure to ensure systems are safe and well governed.
“As AI agents become more autonomous and embedded in core workflows, companies can’t afford to have unmanaged AI operating inside their business.,” she said.
“Aigentsphere gives leaders a clear line of sight and control over how AI is being used, the impact on costs, the business performance, and the risks it introduces. Aigentsphere enables companies to truly understand their ROI for agents, not just as a theoretical exercise in development cycles, but in an ongoing sustainable way throughout the life span of the active agents.”

As enterprises accelerate adoption of generative AI and autonomous agents, many encounter what Aigentsphere describes as “agent sprawl”, an issue that occurs when disconnected and untracked systems create cost inefficiencies, performance blind spots and compliance risks.
The platform is currently being piloted by several enterprise organisations and is agnostic to underlying AI tech.
CEO Quinton Anderson said it brings the AI-native equivalents of HR and risk management systems to enterprise AI workforces in a centralised system. In one use-case, the Aigentsphere platform identified a legal compliance issue in real time that had escaped the testing and QA process for that agent.
“Enterprises already know how to manage intelligences at scale – they do it every day with sophisticated HR and risk management systems for their human employees. Yet when it comes to AI agents, companies are effectively flying blind,” he said.
“Aigentsphere was built to be the system of record that gives organisations visibility into what their agents are doing, what they cost, and how they’re performing. By bringing structure, accountability and governance to AI, we enable companies to move faster with confidence, not caution.”
Main Sequence partner Mike Zimmerman said agentic AI is a significant shift in enterprise technology.
“AI moving from simple prediction tasks to autonomous systems executing complex workflows at scale. But that transition only succeeds if organisations can manage, monitor and govern what their agents are actually doing,” he said.
“Aigentsphere is building the critical infrastructure that makes that possible, giving enterprises the visibility and accountability they need to move from AI experimentation to confident, enterprise-wide deployment.”
