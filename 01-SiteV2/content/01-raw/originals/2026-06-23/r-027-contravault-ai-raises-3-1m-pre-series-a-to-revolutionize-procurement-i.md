# ContraVault AI Raises $3.1M Pre-Series A to Revolutionize Procurement Intelligence

ContraVault AI Raises $3.1M Pre-Series A to Revolutionize Procurement Intelligence
Leave a Comment
• By
Swapnil Gupta
• June 17, 2026
Infrastructure runs the world. Roads, power grids, defense systems, bridges – none of it happens without someone winning a contract first. And winning a contract means going through procurement. It means tenders, RFPs, compliance checks, risk assessments, and bid submissions. It means weeks of manual work, spreadsheets, and gut calls.
Most large infrastructure companies are still doing this the hard way. ContraVault AI decided that was unacceptable.
The Bengaluru-based startup just closed a $3.1 million pre-Series A funding round. But the money is almost secondary to what it represents. This is the market finally waking up to a problem that has been hiding in plain sight for decades.
What Is ContraVault AI and What Does It Do
ContraVault AI is a procurement intelligence platform built for infrastructure, construction, energy, power, defense, and aerospace companies. Founded in 2024 by Sayan Sen, Isha Juneja, and Tanmay Juneja, the company was built around a very specific frustration: the tender bidding process is one of the most complex, high-stakes workflows in enterprise, and almost nobody had tried to fix it properly.
Here is the kicker. When an infrastructure company wants to win a contract, it does not just submit a form. It has to discover the right tenders from thousands published daily. Analyze dense, technical documents. Evaluate commercial risk. Check compliance. Decide whether to even bid. Then actually prepare and submit the bid. Every step of this, at most large companies, is done by hand.
ContraVault AI sits inside that entire workflow. The platform helps organizations discover opportunities, analyze tender documents and RFPs, flag risks, check compliance, make faster go/no-go decisions, and prepare submissions with far less manual effort.
And the numbers behind the product are not small. The platform has analyzed over one million tenders as part of its training dataset. It is already used by some of India’s largest infrastructure companies, including Voltas, Shapoorji Pallonji, Adani Group, and Sterling and Wilson. Around 40 customers in India. Customer base doubled in the last two months.
But the metric that actually matters? ContraVault AI helps clients cut tender processing time by 60 to 70 percent. And increases the number of bids submitted by 30 to 35 percent. That is not a productivity story. That is a revenue story.
ContraVault AI Raises $3.1M Pre-Series A Led by Chiratae Ventures
The $3.1 million Pre-Series A round was led by Chiratae Ventures. Titan Capital Winners Fund, an existing backer, also participated.
This is not the first money ContraVault AI has raised. Before this round, the company had pulled in roughly $600,000 from Titan Capital and angel investors at the seed stage. So the total funding now sits at approximately $3.7 million. Not bad for a company that did not exist two years ago.
Let’s be honest about what Chiratae leading this round actually signals. Procurement intelligence is not a sexy category. It does not trend on social media. It does not have viral growth loops. It is deep, unglamorous, domain-specific enterprise software. The kind of software that solves a real problem for people who actually need it. And Chiratae saw that clearly.
Sayan Sen said it best: “Infrastructure is the largest industry known to humankind that still relies on Excel sheets and manual intervention for most of its work. Our vision is to give this industry the ability to make decisions that help them win more business through bids and projects. There is a bridge that today’s AI needs to build to carry companies from shallow productivity promises to actual revenue. At ContraVault AI, we are building that bridge.”
That last line is worth sitting with. Shallow productivity promises. That is most of what the AI market is selling right now. ContraVault AI is betting that infrastructure companies want something more. Based on the client results, they are right.
How ContraVault AI Is Changing the Tender Bidding Process
The tender bidding process does not just have inefficiencies. It is structurally broken.
Most infrastructure companies find out about relevant tenders late. They analyze documents slowly. Risk decisions get made informally, based on experience and instinct. Bids get submitted with errors. Good tenders get missed entirely. Bad ones get chased. The cost of all this, in time and lost contracts, is enormous.
ContraVault AI is rebuilding this process using AI trained specifically on procurement. Not a general-purpose tool with a procurement-themed interface. A platform that has ingested over a million real tenders, understands the specific language of infrastructure documents, and delivers outputs that fit inside how enterprise teams already work.
The platform covers the full cycle. Tender discovery. Document analysis. Risk evaluation. Compliance checks. Go/no-go recommendations. Bid preparation. It does not ask teams to change their workflow. The product adapts to them. That distinction matters more than most people realize.
And the sectors it serves are not niche. Construction, energy, power, defense, aerospace. These are backbone industries. In India alone, public infrastructure spending runs into tens of lakhs of crore every year. Every single project triggers a procurement process that someone has to navigate. Right now, most of them are navigating it with spreadsheets and prayer.
So when ContraVault AI says it is changing the tender bidding process, take that seriously. The company has already expanded beyond tender analysis into a broader bid management platform. It has built real relationships with India’s leading EPC companies. The product is working.
ContraVault AI Plans US Expansion and Product Growth with New Funding
The $3.1 million is not going to sit idle. ContraVault AI has a clear plan, and it is moving on three fronts simultaneously.
First, the United States. US infrastructure spending is surging, driven by federal investments in clean energy, roads, and defense. The procurement and bidding process there is just as manual, just as complex, and just as broken as it is in India. The market fit is direct. This is not ContraVault AI trying something new. It is taking what already works and applying it to a bigger arena.
Second, the product. The company is investing in fine-tuning domain-specific large language models built for procurement intelligence. These are not off-the-shelf models. They are built to handle complex tender analysis, commercial risk evaluation, compliance workflows, and bid decisioning at a level of accuracy that requires genuine domain depth. Generic tools cannot get there. That is the whole point.
Third, the team. Scaling into the US while deepening the India product requires people. The new capital creates the runway to hire without slowing down.
The reality is, ContraVault AI is not trying to be everything to everyone. It is going deep into one of the most complex, high-value enterprise workflows in existence and owning it completely. Every tender the platform analyzes makes the model smarter. Every smarter model delivers better outcomes for clients. Better outcomes make the product stickier. It is a compounding advantage that takes years to build and is very hard to copy.
Infrastructure procurement is a problem worth solving. ContraVault AI, with $3.1 million in fresh capital and results that already speak for themselves, is making a serious run at it.
