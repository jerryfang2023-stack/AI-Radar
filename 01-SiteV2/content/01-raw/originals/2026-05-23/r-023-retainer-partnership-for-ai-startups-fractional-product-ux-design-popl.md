Retainer Partnership
Get a fractional design lead embedded in your team—shipping UX, landing pages, and design systems every month so you stop leaking users and time.
From $1,500/month · 10–15 hrs/month
Instead of chasing freelancers or overloading your PMs with Figma , you get one partner who owns product UX , conversion journeys, and visual consistency across your AI product and marketing.
Schedule Free Strategy Call
AI feature UI — chat/generation panel with trust indicators
Built for AI founders who need a design adult in the room
This retainer is for AI startup teams who have a live product, constant ideas, and not enough design capacity to ship them without chaos. You’re moving fast, but your onboarding , pricing , and UX are a Frankenstein of past experiments.​
If you’re pre‑seed to Series B and juggling roadmap, investor decks, and landing pages, this gives you an embedded senior partner who speaks product, not just pixels.​
Ideal fit:
AI startup with a live product or beta and a real roadmap, not just an idea.​
No in‑house design lead, or a small team stretched across product and growth.​
Frequent “small but critical” design tasks: new flows, onboarding, pricing tests, launch pages.​
You care about metrics: activation , retention, LTV, and fundraising outcomes—not Dribbble shots.
What your design partner actually does
This is an ongoing engagement focused on moving metrics, not burning hours.
Product UX
UX reviews and “friction clean‑ups” across onboarding, core flows, and dashboards.​
New feature flows, wireframes, and production‑ready UI in Figma. ​
Design system upkeep and extension for new patterns and components.​
Growth & marketing design
Conversion‑focused landing pages and campaign pages in Figma + Framer/Lovable where needed.​
In‑product promos, launch visuals, and simple email/ad creative aligned with your brand.​
Testing support: variants for pricing, messaging, and UX experiments tied to your analytics.​
Strategy & feedback loops
Weekly async or live strategy touchpoint for prioritization and decision‑making.​
Monthly mini‑retro: what moved, what didn’t, and where to focus next. ​
Input across product, marketing, and fundraising decks to keep the story coherent.​
Simple workflow. Clear boundaries. No drama.
Step 1 – Kickoff & audit
In week 0, we run a 60–90 minute workshop to align on goals, metrics, roadmap, and tools (Figma, Slack, Linear, Notion , HubSpot, etc.). You also get a rapid audit of your product UX, funnels, and visual system to spot quick wins.​
Step 2 – Monthly focus and backlog
Each month we pick 1–3 key outcomes: improve activation, launch a feature, redesign a landing page, or stabilize your design system. All requests live in a shared backlog; you can add ideas anytime, and work is pulled in based on hours and impact.​
Step 3 – Communication
Day‑to‑day is async‑first via Slack/Discord with 24–48h response time on business days. Weekly Loom or live calls keep decisions moving without killing your calendar.​
Step 4 – Delivery
Everything ships in organized Figma files with Loom walkthroughs and implementation notes. For landing pages, we can either hand off designs or ship directly in Framer/Lovable with basic tracking wired in.​​

FAQs about the retainer partnership
How many requests can we send per month?
You can submit as many requests as you want; work is pulled from a prioritized backlog based on your plan’s hours and the impact on your metrics.​
What’s the minimum commitment?
The initial commitment is 3 months so there’s enough runway to audit, prioritize, and ship meaningful improvements, then we move to a simple month‑to‑month model.​
Can we pause the retainer?
You can pause once per year for up to one month if you hit a funding or roadmap freeze; your slot is held, but hours do not accumulate. ​
How is this different from hiring a freelancer?
Instead of chasing availability for one‑off tasks, you get an embedded partner who understands your product, team, and stack—and is accountable for outcomes, not just delivery.​
What’s not included?
No emergency same‑day work, bespoke illustration or video production, or backend development; for front‑end builds, delivery is focused on Framer/Lovable and your existing no‑code stack.
