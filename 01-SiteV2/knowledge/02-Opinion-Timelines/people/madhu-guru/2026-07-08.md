> 真的。让我们解开这个。。。人们认为数据和evals是低技能的，你可以买到的咕噜咕噜的工作，并将其放入模型中。在实践中，模型生命周期看起来像模型策略- > evals - >前/后培训/rl与evals一致- > GTM。像任何产品策略一样，你需要预先提出强烈的意见，但由于建立一个擅长所有事情的LLM的吸引力，它要困难100倍。 下一个挑战是，通过最新架构、回归、竞争数据贡献和最新竞争对手新闻的挑战，在整个模型构建过程中始终专注于目标评估集。然后，您可以在多个检查点之间做出选择，以目标回收、回归回收和早期客户反馈进行交易。所以，是的，它始于通过回收表达的意见，如果你执行得好，你就会落在目标附近。

**原文摘录**

> true. let’s unpack this..people think data and evals are low skill, grunt work that you can just buy and stuff into models. in practice, the model lifecycle looks something like model strategy -> evals -> pre/post training/rl aligned to evals -> GTM. like any product strategy, you need strong opinions up front but it is 100x harder because of the allure of building an LLM that is great at everything. the next challenge is staying focused on the target eval sets throughout the model build through challenges with the latest architecture, regressions, competing data contributions and the latest competitor news. you then make choices between multiple checkpoints trading off on target evals, regression evals and early customer feedback. so yes, it begins with an opinion expressed through evals and if you execute well, you land somewhere near your target.
