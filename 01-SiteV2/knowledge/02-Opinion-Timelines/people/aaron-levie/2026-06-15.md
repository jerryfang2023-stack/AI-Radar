**中文翻译**

> 说“我们需要一个AI领域的FDA”或类似的政府机构，听起来很简单。但实际情况会是这样的。AI模型的能力几乎有无穷无尽的排列组合。要想在每次模型发布前，都有一套纯粹客观、普遍适用的衡量标准，而不经过模型实验室、学术界和政府之间漫长的反复沟通、研究和辩论，将非常困难。现在，再想象一下，在全球范围内，你与每个有业务往来的国家，对每一次模型发布都要经历同样的流程。再加上数十个AI模型发布积压在一起，你很快就能看到，这最终会极大地拖慢所有AI的进步。这就是为什么我们需要主要关注对AI实际应用场景的监管，因为风险正是在那里显现的。

**原文摘录**

> 原始观点/摘要：It’s very easy to say “we need an FDA for AI” or some equivalent government agency. Well this is what that would look like. The capabilities of AI models have near infinite permutations. It’s going to be very hard have a purely objective set of metrics that can be universally applied before every model release, without extended back and forth, research, and debate between model labs, academics, and the government. Now, imagine this same process with every country that you’re doing business with, globally, for every single model release. Add in a backlog of dozens of AI model releases, and you can quickly see how in the limit this will dramatically slow down all AI progress.
