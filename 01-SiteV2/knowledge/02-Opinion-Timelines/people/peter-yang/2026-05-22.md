> 我讨厌看到这些大规模裁员。作为员工，你可以做以下6件事来重新掌控局面：
> 1. 读懂信号
> 如果业务增长停滞，领导层突然痴迷于“扁平化组织”或“为智能体时代重组”，你其实已经知道接下来会发生什么。
> 2. 学习Codex或Claude Code
> 这些应用是与AI智能体协作的最佳训练场。告诉它们你的工作内容，问问你能构建什么。如果公司不让你用这些工具，就在个人项目中使用它们。
> 3. 打造副业项目
> 发布一个小产品，或为开源项目做贡献。如果你在大公司工作，你的构建能力可能已经退化。是时候把它们找回来了。
> 4. 积累GitHub历史记录
> 如果你持续执行第3步，久而久之就会在GitHub上积累一批作品。我的朋友@zarazhangrui就是一个很好的例子——她开发了一个前端幻灯片技能，目前在GitHub上获得了16K星标，并且一直在持续发布。
> 5. 在你的技艺中跻身前10%
> AI能让人很快达到平均水平。但这恰恰意味着客户更愿意为人类的技艺和品味支付溢价。选一项你真正喜欢钻研的技能，反复练习，直到你在这项技能上进入前10%。
> 6. 让市场决定你的价值
> 公开构建，解决真实用户的问题，精心打造优秀的产品。这样你才会被优秀的雇主注意到，而不是靠投递100份简历。如果所有方法都行不通，不妨考虑成为

**原文摘录**

> I hate seeing all the mass layoffs. Here are 6 things you can do as an employee to take back control: 1. Read the signals If business growth is stagnant and leadership is suddenly obsessed with "flatter orgs" or "restructuring for the agentic era," you already know what's coming. 2. Learn Codex or Claude Code These apps are the best training grounds for working with AI agents. Tell them about your job and ask them what you can build. If your company doesn’t let you use these tools, use them for your personal projects. 3. Build side projects Ship a small product or make an open-source contribution. If you work at a large company, your builder skills have probably atrophied. It’s time to bring them back. 4. Develop a GitHub history If you do step 3 consistently, you’ll develop a body of work on GitHub over time. My friend @zarazhangrui is a great example — she built a frontend-slides skill that now has 16K GitHub stars and has been shipping non-stop since. 5. Become top 10% at your craft AI gets people to average really fast. But that just means customers are willing to pay more for human craft and taste. Pick one skill you genuinely enjoy working on and put in the reps until you’re in the top 10% at it. 6. Let the market determine your value Build in public, solve real user problems, and craft great products. That’s how you’ll get noticed by great employers, not by submitting 100s of resumes. And if all else fails, consider becoming a founder. I think entrepreneurship is the safest job in the AI era anyway. Practical AI tutorials and interviews for busy people |
