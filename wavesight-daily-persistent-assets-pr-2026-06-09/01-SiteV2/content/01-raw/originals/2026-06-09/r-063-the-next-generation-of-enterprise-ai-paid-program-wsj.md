# The Next Generation of Enterprise AI - Paid Program - WSJ

## clean_text

As generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) becomes a more critical means to enhance a wide range of business tasks, many companies are deploying it at edge locations—from factory floors to retail stores to hospitals.
But as companies push their applications to the edge, they are finding that traditional enterprise network architectures were not built for GenAI’s rapidly shifting traffic flows and uneven bandwidth needs. The ability to dynamically and intelligently prioritize both applications and the connections they run on becomes key to good business outcomes.
Running GenAI at the edge can offer advantages over running AI models in the cloud, including lower latency and cost, better performance and improved data security. The problem, says Tilly Gilbert, consulting and edge practice lead at STL Partners, is that many companies still lack the network infrastructure for AI deployment, especially at the edge. “A lot of enterprises today rely on what we call ‘best effort’ connectivity, which essentially means they don’t differentiate between the types of traffic they have,” she says. “So if the network quality degrades or drops out, you can’t do much about it.”
With more companies relying on GenAI for vital tasks that depend on consistent data feeds, network reliability has become a strategic imperative. A brief stutter in a video call, for example, is merely annoying while a stutter in a GenAI application can break it completely. “As enterprises adopt more mission-critical AI applications, they can’t afford to just deal with it not working sometimes,” she adds.
The Value of Convergence
One solution to these challenges, says Sanjay Uppal, vice president and general manager of the VeloCloud division at global technology leader Broadcom, is to combine the strengths of multiple connectivity solutions—including wired, wireless and satellite networks—using software-defined wide area networks (SD-WAN) to optimize their use. VeloCloud is Broadcom’s SD-WAN solution that helps manage enterprise networks, including highly distributed networks with edge computing.
As new GenAI applications get rolled out, a converged network allows enterprises to maintain consistent performance and security at a lower cost.
“There isn’t a single networking technology—5G, fixed wireless access, fiber, satellite—that does everything perfectly and is available everywhere,” Uppal says. “Convergence allows us to take the different types of networks and combine them on behalf of high-demand applications.”
Convergence enables companies to tailor connectivity to application needs, ensuring critical workloads receive appropriate resources. It offers advantages over traditional quality-of-service solutions by providing a unified infrastructure for managing different traffic types, simplifying network management and reducing costs. It also scales more easily as network demands increase dramatically with AI adoption.
“It’s increasingly important to look at the needs of each application based on the performance demands on the network,” Uppal says. “Generative AI is now touching practically every class of applications, with the next big revolution being AI agents that are extremely demanding of the network and are often going to be deployed at the edge. So this all makes convergence a more critical and valuable approach.”
Making Intelligent Decisions
As enterprises implement a converged network strategy, they must first identify their users and critical applications. This information drives optimal network design to prevent disruptions.
5G, satellite, broadband and fiber each have unique characteristics. With these technologies combined, you can leverage the strengths of each to support demanding AI applications.
Sanjay Uppal,
VP and General Manager, VeloCloud Division, Broadcom
“From that information, you can derive the right network architecture to run these applications effectively at a reasonable cost,” Uppal says.
The growth of GenAI at the edge will also change network demands, as it requires more upstream bandwidth as devices collect and upload data for on-site analysis, Uppal explains. For example, a smart mirror in a retail store may provide AI-generated product recommendations—something possible only after the retailer uploads data generated by the mirror to its edge servers for analysis. AI deployments also introduce greater security concerns due to sensitive data collection, and AI agents often need secure channels to communicate with other tools.
This means companies will constantly need help as they continuously optimize their networks. “You can’t have armies of people running around managing these networks,” he says. A converged network with an intelligent overlay—software that dynamically manages traffic across multiple connection types—provides the flexibility in connectivity options along with the automation of optimization that can solve this dilemma.
“5G, satellite, broadband and fiber each have unique characteristics,” Uppal adds. “With these technologies combined, you can leverage the strengths of each to support demanding AI applications.”
Once appropriate connectivity is established at each location, businesses must ensure their most vital applications receive priority. Healthcare providers might prioritize diagnostic tools while retailers prioritize point-of-sale systems—whatever is most critical to that business.
Broadcom’s VeloCloud analyzes application performance requirements and automatically optimizes connectivity in real time. Using its proprietary Dynamic Multipath Optimization (DMPO) technology, it determines which applications are most business-critical and prioritizes that traffic accordingly. “It basically determines how to take an application and use the network in the best possible way,” Uppal says.
VeloCloud also allows businesses to customize application prioritization policies, though about 80% of companies use the default settings because the software is so effective at determining which applications those should be, Uppal says. “We have more than 4,300 applications that we can identify and tend to know from experience which applications should be prioritized,” he adds.
Gilbert says that as GenAI adoption at the edge grows, network convergence along with application prioritization will help companies ensure their key applications work seamlessly—enhancing customer and employee experiences and handling valuable tasks without the risk of costly disruptions.
“It’s no longer just about whether applications work smoothly most of the time,” she adds. “Now, they have to work all of the time.”
