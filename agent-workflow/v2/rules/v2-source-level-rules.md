# V2 Source Level Rules

| Level | Definition | Can Support |
|---|---|---|
| S | First-party or primary evidence: official blog, product page, docs, pricing, filings, customer case, repository | Fact evidence, source anchor |
| A | High-trust secondary source: quality media, investor report, research, public procurement, financing database | Fact enhancement, background |
| B | Ecosystem source: Product Hunt, YC, GitHub, HN, vertical community, podcast | Early signal, ecosystem heat |
| C | Social or aggregation source: X, LinkedIn, Reddit, listicles, single personal view | Trigger, calibration, counter-evidence |

## Rules

- C-level sources cannot be sole fact evidence.
- Builder views can calibrate heat and expose disagreement, but cannot replace source verification.
- Front Signals require at least 3 S/A/B sources.
- Deep Dives require at least 5 sources, including at least 2 S-level or direct sources when available.
- Source reliability notes should be recorded in Source Registry before recurring use.
